## Summary
Ye et al. built a wearable, inkjet-printed aptamer patch that quantifies oestradiol from sweat by reading the methylene-blue redox signal on AuNP-MXene electrodes. It pushes electrochemical aptamer sensing toward low-abundance hormones in a low-cost, mass-producible wearable rather than a lab instrument. It grounds the electrochemical-aptamer-biosensing primitive, though it measures sweat non-invasively rather than the implantable in-vivo sensing the source hint implied.
