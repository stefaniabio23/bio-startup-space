## Summary
Yu et al. showed that full-length proteins can be captured and moved single-file and unidirectionally through a solid-state nanopore using electro-osmotic flow, without the enzymatic unfolding previously required. Slow, controlled transport of intact proteins is a prerequisite for reading their current fingerprints, moving nanopore sensing from DNA toward single-molecule protein analysis. It grounds the nanopore-sensing primitive on the protein-readout frontier.
