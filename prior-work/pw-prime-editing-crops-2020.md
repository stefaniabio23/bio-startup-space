## Summary
Lin et al. adapted prime editing to plants, using codon, promoter, and condition optimisation to install donor-free, double-strand-break-free point mutations, insertions, and deletions in rice and wheat, regenerating edited rice at up to ~21.8%. It demonstrated the search-and-replace prime-editing mechanism generalises well beyond mammalian cells. It grounds the prime-base-editing primitive, though the founding mammalian platform paper is Anzalone et al. 2019.
