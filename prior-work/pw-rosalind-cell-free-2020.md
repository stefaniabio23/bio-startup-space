## Summary
The ROSALIND platform uses allosteric transcription factors to gate an in vitro transcription reaction that produces a fluorescent RNA aptamer output, freeze-dried for field detection of water contaminants. It shows cell-free genetic circuits can be lyophilised into shelf-stable, equipment-light biosensors for specific small molecules. This is a foundational demonstration for the cell-free-synthetic-biology primitive.
