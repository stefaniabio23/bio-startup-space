## Idea

Chronic inflammatory disease is driven by a history of triggers, but current diagnostics only give a snapshot at the moment of sampling. Engineer a commensal gut bacterium to host a DNA molecular recorder that writes a time-ordered log of exposures (inflammatory signals, metabolites) as it transits the gut. Sequence the recovered bacteria afterward and reconstruct the sequence of events preceding a Crohn's or ulcerative-colitis flare, a temporal record no biopsy or blood test provides.

## Why now

Two halves matured independently and can now be joined. DNA Typewriter and ENGRAM became general, temporally resolved, multi-event molecular recorders through 2025-2026 protocol publications, and programmable commensal bacteria that sense long-term gut inflammation were demonstrated in mice. The recorder and the in-vivo sensor now both exist; nobody has fused them for IBD.

## 3-month MVP

Demonstrate in vitro that an engineered strain couples an inflammation-linked input to a DNA-recorder write event, and that sequencing recovers the correct order of two sequential stimuli. Pair this with a strain-safety and biocontainment plan. The output is a proof that sense-to-record coupling works at all, the riskiest scientific assumption, before any animal or clinical work.

## Validation plan

Show an engineered strain writes a time-ordered DNA record of a defined inducer in the mouse gut and that the log is recoverable by sequencing after transit, establishing durable in-vivo temporal recording.

## Notes

Very early (TRL 2) and science-timeline, so frame it as an IBD-mechanism research tool and a pharma data-licensing play rather than a near-term diagnostic. A live engineered gut organism carries a high regulatory bar. Weak EF fit; suits a synthetic-biology lab spinout.
