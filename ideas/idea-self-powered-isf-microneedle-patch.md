## Idea
A microneedle patch reaches interstitial fluid painlessly and reads a panel of analytes continuously, glucose, lactate, electrolytes, drug levels, and inflammatory markers, rather than glucose alone. Adding triboelectric harvesting shrinks the battery and reduces charging. The wedge is a specific high-value clinical need, remote heart-failure or endocrine monitoring, where a continuous non-glucose panel changes management.

## Why now
Microneedle ISF sensing and continuous aptamer readout both matured, and remote patient-monitoring reimbursement now pays for continuous trends beyond glucose, so a multi-analyte ISF patch has both the technical basis and a paying buyer.

## 3-month MVP
Build a microneedle patch with one non-glucose channel (an electrolyte or a drug level), run it in an ISF-mimicking skin phantom over 24-72 hours, and characterize calibration, drift, and biofouling. Separately estimate the fraction of the energy budget triboelectric harvesting can supply.

## Validation plan
Demonstrate a stable, calibrated continuous readout of one clinically meaningful non-glucose analyte from ISF over multi-day duration, the proof that the panel extends beyond commoditized glucose.

## Notes
Win by owning a specific clinical use case with evidence, not by out-featuring CGM incumbents. The heart-failure electrolyte/drug-level angle is the sharpest wedge.
