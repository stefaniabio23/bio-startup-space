## Idea
Molecular diagnostics normally need a lab, instruments, and a cold chain. Freeze-dried cell-free reactions put the molecular logic onto a cheap paper strip that ships and stores at room temperature and runs when you add the sample, producing a visual result. It computes a diagnostic answer, detecting a pathogen's nucleic acid or a biomarker, without refrigeration or equipment. The wedge is a specific target where instrument-free, cold-chain-free testing is decisive.

## Why now
Freeze-dried paper-based cell-free sensors (toehold switches) detected viral RNA at low cost without instruments, lyophilization is established as the route to room-temperature reactions, and demand for decentralized molecular testing stayed strong after the pandemic.

## 3-month MVP
Build a freeze-dried cell-free strip for one target, run a limit-of-detection and specificity panel against relevant off-targets, and, critically, test performance after accelerated room-temperature storage to prove the reaction reactivates reliably. The deliverable is a shelf-stable strip with characterized sensitivity.

## Validation plan
Demonstrate detection of one target at clinically relevant concentration with a clear readout after realistic storage, the proof that cold-chain-free operation does not cost accuracy.

## Notes
Most mature and clearest-need idea in the set. Win with a specific decisive target and a distribution/regulatory partner, not a general programmable-platform pitch against Sherlock and Mammoth.
