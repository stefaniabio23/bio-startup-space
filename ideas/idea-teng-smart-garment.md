## Idea
An athletic garment turns the wearer's own movement into electricity through fabric friction and uses sweat flow to power local chemistry sensors, so the garment needs no battery. It logs movement load for training and reads sweat biochemistry (electrolytes, lactate) during exercise. The wedge is being genuinely charge-free and launderable, which is exactly where prior smart apparel with rigid modules and coin cells fell down.

## Why now
Textile triboelectric harvesting from body motion and self-powered in-garment glucose sensing were both demonstrated, and connected-fitness apparel is an established consumer category, so a battery-free garment has both the technical basis and a market.

## 3-month MVP
Fabricate a triboelectric garment swatch, quantify energy harvested across running and cycling motion, run one full sensing-and-transmit cycle on that harvested energy, and test durability and output retention across repeated wash cycles.

## Validation plan
Show a launderable garment section that harvests enough energy from real running motion to power and transmit one sensor reading, the core self-powered claim.

## Notes
This is a manufacturing and durability bet more than a science bet. Smart apparel is a crowded graveyard; only durable, launderable, truly charge-free fabric earns a place.
