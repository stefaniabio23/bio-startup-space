## Idea
A single microneedle patch does two jobs at once: it delivers a drug or vaccine and it deposits an invisible near-infrared quantum-dot pattern in the dermis that encodes the health record. The pattern is unreadable to the eye but readable by a modified near-infrared camera, so the medical record travels with the dose and survives without paper or connectivity. The target is immunization in settings where record-keeping infrastructure does not exist.

## Why now
The MIT quantum-dot microneedle record was demonstrated in animal models with patterns readable nine months after delivery and co-delivered antigen producing protective immunity, and global-health funders are actively backing offline record infrastructure.

## 3-month MVP
Fabricate a microneedle patch that co-delivers a model payload and a near-infrared quantum-dot pattern into synthetic or ex-vivo skin, then demonstrate camera readout, pattern fidelity, and early persistence over a few weeks. The deliverable is a reliably readable encoded pattern.

## Validation plan
Reproduce a readable quantum-dot pattern in a skin model after co-delivery and characterize readout robustness and persistence, the core feasibility questions before any safety work.

## Notes
The hard barriers are regulatory, safety, and social acceptance of an under-skin marker, not the optics. This is an institutional global-health play more than an EF solo-founder bet.
