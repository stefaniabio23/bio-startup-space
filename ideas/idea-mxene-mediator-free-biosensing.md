## Idea

Mediated electrochemical sensors depend on a diffusible redox shuttle that ages, drifts, and complicates the chemistry. MXene's high conductivity and large surface area let the enzyme hand electrons straight to the electrode, so the mediator disappears. Fewer components means longer shelf life, simpler manufacturing chemistry, and a cleaner regulatory story for glucose, lactate, and similar enzymatic assays.

## Why now

2025 review articles consolidated a decade of MXene electrochemical sensing into design principles and confirmed MXene serving as active interfacial sites that mediate rapid direct electron transfer in enzymatic sensors. The science has matured past one-off demos, so the open problem shifts to reproducible, stable manufacturing, a founder-shaped gap.

## 3-month MVP

Fabricate a reproducible mediator-free MXene enzymatic electrode for glucose or lactate and benchmark it head-to-head against a commercial mediated strip on sensitivity and drift. Run an accelerated-aging study to quantify the MXene oxidation problem and test one mitigation (surface passivation or alloying). The deliverable is a stability curve that either validates or kills the thesis.

## Validation plan

Measure sensitivity, drift, and shelf life of the mediator-free MXene electrode against a commercial reference on the same analyte, with oxidation stability as the make-or-break metric.

## Notes

The academic space is crowded, so the moat is not the sensing demo but a route to oxidation-stable MXene at reproducible commercial quality. Without that manufacturing edge this is a paper, not a company.
