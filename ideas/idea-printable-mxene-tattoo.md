## Idea
A biosensor printed like a temporary tattoo, but with MXene as the active material, gives high surface area and conductivity for sensitive, multiplexed sweat chemistry at disposable cost. It reads a panel (glucose, lactate, electrolytes) continuously from a skin-conformal printed film. The differentiation is the material: MXene can out-sensitize and out-multiplex carbon or enzyme-only tattoo sensors, if it can be kept stable in sweat.

## Why now
MXene electrochemical sweat sensors for glucose, cortisol, and other analytes were demonstrated across 2023-2024, and printed epidermal tattoo platforms are established, so a printable MXene tattoo is a near-term integration of two proven pieces.

## 3-month MVP
Formulate a functionalized MXene ink, print a two-analyte tattoo sensor, and characterize sensitivity and cross-talk. The load-bearing measurement is oxidative signal drift in artificial and human sweat over 8-24 hours, since MXene stability in biofluid is the core translational gap.

## Validation plan
Demonstrate a printed MXene tattoo holding calibrated multi-analyte readout over a realistic wear window without oxidative signal collapse, the proof the material advantage survives contact with sweat.

## Notes
Oxidative stability and scalable functionalized-ink manufacturing are the whole game. Solving them defensibly is a real materials moat; the open question is whether sweat sensing has a strong enough clinical or consumer value case at all.
