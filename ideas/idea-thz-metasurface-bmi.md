## Idea

Penetrating microelectrode BMIs trade tissue damage for signal precision. A reconfigurable metasurface reframes the interface as an electromagnetic field-shaping problem: a thin, software-controlled surface that steers stimulation patterns across neural tissue without wires or penetrating shanks, reprogrammable in software rather than fixed at fabrication. Terahertz and metamaterial metasurfaces are the substrate that could make this spatially precise.

## Why now

Through 2025 a cluster of reviews and device papers, notably in Light: Science & Applications and Research, articulated metasurface-assisted bioelectronics for retinal, cochlear, and cortical implants, with FPGA-controlled reconfigurable patches and phase-change-material tuning. This is a newly named research direction rather than a product, which is exactly why a founder-scientist could stake early IP.

## 3-month MVP

Combine electromagnetic simulation with a benchtop reconfigurable metasurface that demonstrates dynamic field/beam steering in the target band. Deliver a tissue-safety power budget and a concrete in-vitro protocol for coupling the field to a neuron culture. The goal is a credible feasibility case and a provisional patent, not a device.

## Validation plan

Demonstrate reconfigurable field steering at biologically safe power, then couple the metasurface to an in-vitro neuronal culture and record evoked electrical activity as first proof the modality can influence neurons at all.

## Notes

This is a moonshot with a decade-plus regulatory horizon and deep physics risk on THz tissue penetration. It suits a Cambridge photonics-lab spinout with patient IP-licensing capital, not an EF cofounder-pairing sprint. Score reflects scientific interest against near-term company viability.
