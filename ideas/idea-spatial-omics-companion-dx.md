## Idea

Immunotherapy decisions still lean on PD-L1 IHC, a coarse single-marker snapshot that misses the spatial organisation of the tumour immune microenvironment. 3D spatial multi-omics captures how immune and tumour cells are arranged in space, which carries the real predictive signal for checkpoint response. Productise that into a standardised companion diagnostic: one tumour section in, a validated responder/non-responder call out, better than PD-L1.

## Why now

3D spatial platforms crossed from niche to scaled and capitalised in 2024-2025, with Stellaromics raising an $80M Series B in February 2025 to commercialise its Pyxa 3D platform and 10x Genomics expanding Xenium. Spatial biology is being adopted across oncology drug discovery, which builds the reference data and clinical momentum a companion diagnostic needs. The instruments now exist; the clinical-evidence and assay layer is the open opportunity.

## 3-month MVP

Run a retrospective analysis on public or partnered spatial datasets to derive a tumour-microenvironment signature that stratifies checkpoint-inhibitor responders. Report a head-to-head AUC against PD-L1 IHC and outline a concrete CLIA assay-development path. This tests the core claim, that spatial architecture beats the incumbent marker, using existing data before any wet-lab or trial spend.

## Validation plan

Show a spatial immune-architecture signature predicts immunotherapy response on a retrospective annotated cohort with outcomes, beating PD-L1 IHC on AUC, the result that justifies building the assay and pursuing CDx validation.

## Notes

Do not build another spatial instrument against 10x, NanoString/Bruker, and Stellaromics. The defensible wedge is the assay-standardisation, computational signature, and clinical-evidence layer that turns their instruments into a reimbursable companion diagnostic. Access to annotated outcome cohorts is the real moat. Plausible across founder, Cambridge, and EF routes if paired with a computational-pathology cofounder.
