## Idea

Electrically active tissues (heart, nerve, muscle) need scaffolds that both host cells and carry electrical signals, but today's bio-inks are structural and non-conductive. A MXene-loaded, self-healing hydrogel is a single printable ink that is conductive, mechanically resilient, and cell-supporting. That enables functional tissue scaffolds and soft bioelectronic tissue interfaces printed in one step, from patch to organ-on-a-chip electrode.

## Why now

MXene conductive and self-healing hydrogel composites matured fast across 2024-2025, with printable supramolecular MXene hydrogels and superhydrophilic MXene-hydrogel wearable substrates demonstrated. The material class is now printable and bio-integrable enough to package as a product, ahead of any standard commercial conductive bio-ink existing.

## 3-month MVP

Formulate a reproducible, sterilisable MXene bio-ink and characterise its printability, through-scaffold conductivity, and 7-to-14-day cytocompatibility against a non-conductive control. Add an accelerated-oxidation study to establish shelf life. This produces a sellable research consumable and a data pack, independent of any implant ambitions.

## Validation plan

Print a conductive MXene hydrogel scaffold, measure cell viability over one-to-two weeks and quantify through-scaffold conductivity versus control, the two properties a customer buys the ink for.

## Notes

The bioprinting tools market is small and slow, so the pragmatic wedge is selling a validated conductive, self-healing bio-ink as a research consumable to tissue-engineering and bioelectronics labs, and deferring the regulated implant story until the material and demand are proven. Plausible EF fit if paired with a materials cofounder.
