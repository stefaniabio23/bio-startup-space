## One-liner

Public synthetic-biology manufacturer (NASDAQ: TWST) whose silicon platform prints thousands of DNA strands in parallel, supplying synthetic genes, oligo pools, and NGS panels, and a pioneer of DNA data storage.

## Problem

Legacy DNA synthesis uses column or plate chemistry with large reaction volumes, making synthetic genes and oligo pools slow, expensive, and low-throughput, which bottlenecks synthetic biology, antibody discovery, and next-generation sequencing.

## Workaround

Traditional column-based oligo synthesis and 96-well plate gene assembly from other vendors, plus in-house synthesis at large reagent cost.

## Entry wedge

High-throughput synthetic genes and oligo pools at lower cost via chip-based miniaturisation, then expansion into NGS target-enrichment panels, antibody and protein libraries, and DNA data storage.

## Method

A proprietary semiconductor process miniaturises DNA synthesis onto a silicon chip, shrinking reaction volumes roughly a millionfold and raising throughput about a thousandfold, so up to thousands of unique genes or oligos are synthesized in parallel on a single chip.

## Validated by

Founded 2013 by Emily Leproust, Bill Banyai, and Bill Peck; IPO 2018. Fiscal 2026 guidance lifted to about USD 435-440M revenue, twelfth consecutive quarter of growth, with gross margin expanding from roughly 31% to over 53% in two years. Spun out its DNA data-storage business as Atlas Data Storage in May 2025, retaining ~24% equity.

## Competes with

Gene and oligo synthesis providers (IDT/Danaher, GenScript, Ansa Biotechnologies, Molecular Assemblies on enzymatic synthesis) and NGS-panel suppliers (IDT, Agilent).

## Complements

NGS platforms (Illumina, Oxford Nanopore) that consume its panels, antibody and biopharma discovery pipelines, and DNA data-storage efforts (via Atlas Data Storage).

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** semiconductor fabrication, nucleotide reagent suppliers, sequence-design software
**Upstream of:** synthetic-biology R&D, antibody and drug discovery, NGS diagnostics, DNA data storage

## Gaps

Core synthesis is increasingly commoditised, pressuring margins; profitability depends on scaling higher-value NGS and biopharma segments. DNA data storage, once an internal moonshot, was spun out rather than scaled in-house, signalling its long time horizon.
