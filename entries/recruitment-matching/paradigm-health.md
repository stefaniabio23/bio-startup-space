## One-liner

AI-powered clinical trial access platform that combines a national provider network with automated patient matching to make trials part of routine care, focused on oncology.

## Problem

Most cancer patients never learn about a relevant trial. Trials concentrate at a handful of academic centres, so community patients are geographically and structurally cut off, and enrolment routinely runs late and over budget.

## Workaround

Manual chart review, CRO recruitment networks, sponsor site outreach, patient registries, referral to distant academic medical centres.

## Entry wedge

Provider-network aggregation in oncology. Paradigm entered by signing community oncology practices and health systems into a single network, then layering AI patient matching and site feasibility on top, so a sponsor can reach 70% of the US cancer population through one contract rather than dozens of site agreements.

## Method

An AI-enabled platform that automates patient-trial matching and site feasibility across integrated partner data, surfacing eligible patients close to where they already receive care and streamlining site activation for sponsors.

## TAM logic

Clinical trial recruitment and site operations represent roughly $5B+ in annual spend, within a larger clinical development market. Enrolment delays cost sponsors $1M+ per day in lost exclusivity. Paradigm targets the oncology share of that spend by owning the access layer between sponsors and 70% of US cancer patients. (Source: paradigmhealth.ai)

## Validated by

$203M Series A out of stealth (2022), oversubscribed $78M Series B in December 2025 led by ARCH Venture Partners. Supports Phase I-III oncology trials for 15 of the top 20 global pharma companies. Network spans 45 states, 166 provider organisations, 2,100 care locations, and 23 NCI-designated cancer centres. Acquired Flatiron Health's clinical research business.

## Competes with

Tempus (Deep 6 AI), Massive Bio, TrialSpark/Formation Bio, traditional CRO recruitment arms (IQVIA, ICON), site networks and SMOs.

## Complements

EHR vendors (Epic, Cerner), EDC and CTMS platforms (Medidata, Veeva Vault), sponsor clinical operations teams, community oncology practices.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** EHR data, trial protocols, sponsor demand
**Upstream of:** Trial enrolment, site activation, endpoint readout

## Gaps

Solves access and enrolment speed, not biological matching by predicted response or protocol design. Oncology-concentrated. Heavy dependence on maintaining health-system relationships and data-sharing agreements. Capital intensity means the model must sustain high sponsor throughput to justify the network cost.

## Notes

Founder and CEO Kent Thoelke previously led innovation at ICON. Co-incubated by ARCH Venture Partners and General Catalyst. HQ in New York. The 2025 acquisition of Flatiron Health's clinical research business consolidated its oncology trial infrastructure. Total funding reflects $203M Series A plus $78M Series B.
