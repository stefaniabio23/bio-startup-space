## One-liner

AI-native clinical trial matching and navigation embedded in community care, with a health-equity focus that connects underserved and diverse patients to oncology trials.

## Problem

Clinical trials underrepresent non-white, Medicaid, and low-income patients. Eligible community patients are rarely identified, and even when matched, transportation and navigation barriers block enrolment.

## Workaround

Manual site coordinator outreach, academic-centre referral, generic trial-finder websites, CRO recruitment networks that skew toward large urban sites.

## Entry wedge

Community-oncology partnerships with a health-equity wedge. Trial Library embeds in providers such as The Oncology Institute and American Oncology Network, activating clinicians and identifying eligible patients where underserved populations actually receive care, then adding navigation support for transportation and logistics.

## Method

An AI platform that activates providers, screens patients against trial eligibility criteria in community settings, and navigates matched patients through the trial lifecycle, layering equity-focused support services on top of the software.

## TAM logic

Trial recruitment and enrolment spend runs roughly $5B+ annually, with sponsors under regulatory and commercial pressure (FDA diversity guidance) to enrol more representative cohorts. Trial Library targets the community-site and equity-driven share of that spend across sponsor, provider, and payer buyers. (Source: triallibrary.com)

## Validated by

$10M Series A (2025) co-led by SemperVirens and Next Ventures, with Sanofi Ventures, Lux Capital, Civilization Ventures, and How Women Invest, bringing total funding to $15M. Founder Dr. Hala Borno is an oncologist. Reported 642 patient referrals in one year with The Oncology Institute, 69% of referrals non-white, 29% on Medicaid.

## Competes with

Paradigm Health, Tempus (Deep 6 AI), Massive Bio, Mendel, generic trial-matching platforms and CRO recruitment arms.

## Complements

Community oncology providers, EHR vendors, sponsor clinical operations teams, payers seeking to route members to trials as a care option.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Community clinical data, trial eligibility criteria, provider referral
**Upstream of:** Trial enrolment, patient navigation, endpoint readout

## Gaps

Small relative to network-scale competitors like Paradigm. Matching technology is not uniquely defensible. Navigation and equity services are labour-intensive and may pressure margins. Early commercial traction concentrated in a few community-oncology partners.

## Notes
