## One-liner

A two-sided marketplace connecting trial sponsors with a global network of community research sites to run faster, more inclusive clinical trials.

## Problem

Sponsors repeatedly route trials to the same overused academic sites, leaving thousands of capable community sites, and their more diverse patient pools, untapped. Site selection is slow, manual, and biased toward known relationships.

## Workaround

Sponsor and CRO in-house site databases, personal investigator relationships, feasibility questionnaires, incumbent site networks concentrated in urban academic centres.

## Entry wedge

Aggregating the long tail of community research sites. Inato entered by onboarding under-utilised sites and letting them proactively review and apply to trials, giving sponsors access to previously invisible capacity and diverse patient populations through one platform.

## Method

A bidirectional marketplace where sites apply to trials and sponsors screen sites, with AI-assisted site selection, trial planning, and patient pre-screening across 40+ countries and 70+ disease areas.

## TAM logic

Site selection and feasibility sit within roughly $5B+ of annual trial-operations spend, part of a larger clinical development market. Enrolment delays cost sponsors $1M+ per day. Inato monetises the sponsor-site matching layer via SaaS and marketplace fees. (Source: inato.com)

## Validated by

Total funding of approximately $37M, including a $14M Series A (2020) and a $20M Series A2 for international expansion. Platform used by 25+ sponsors and 5,500+ research sites across 40+ countries and 70 disease areas. Based in Paris with US operations.

## Competes with

Paradigm Health, TrialSpark/Formation Bio, Science 37, sponsor and CRO in-house site-selection tools, other site-network and feasibility platforms.

## Complements

CROs running the trials, EDC and CTMS platforms, EHR vendors for site patient data, sponsor clinical operations teams.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Trial protocols, sponsor demand, site capacity data
**Upstream of:** Site activation, patient enrolment, endpoint readout

## Gaps

Marketplace liquidity depends on sustaining both sponsor and site sides. Does not run the trial itself, so value depends on downstream execution by sites and CROs. Modest total funding relative to well-capitalised US competitors like Paradigm. Site quality and consistency across 40+ countries is hard to guarantee.

## Notes

Founded in 2016, headquartered in Paris with US presence. Team of roughly 60. The inclusivity angle aligns with regulatory pressure for more representative trials. Series A2 remains its latest disclosed priced round; total raised approximately $37M.
