## One-liner

Kailera Therapeutics is advancing four clinical-stage GLP-1-based obesity drugs licensed from Hengrui Pharmaceuticals, targeting category-leading weight loss and differentiated tolerability through injectable and oral routes of administration.

## Problem

Despite the arrival of semaglutide and tirzepatide, the obesity epidemic remains: 1B+ people affected globally. Current approved therapies have significant limitations: gastrointestinal side effects limit tolerability and adherence, injections are a barrier for many patients, and weekly injectables deliver 10-25% weight loss but leave a substantial proportion of patients needing greater efficacy. Oral small molecule GLP-1 agonists (oral semaglutide) show lower efficacy than injectables. No approved agent combines superior weight loss with oral convenience.

## Workaround

Ozempic/Wegovy (semaglutide, Novo Nordisk) and Mounjaro/Zepbound (tirzepatide, Eli Lilly) dominate the market. Patients on injectable regimens accept weekly injections. Others use off-label compounded semaglutide. Oral semaglutide (Rybelsus) offers convenience with lower efficacy.

## Entry wedge

Ribupatide (KAI-9531): once-weekly injectable GLP-1/GIP dual agonist in global Phase 3. Lead program builds on Phase 2 data from Hengrui's China trials, providing a head-start on global program design. Presented at ADA 2026. Clinical data update Q1 2026: all four programs progressing.

## Method

Portfolio licensing strategy: Kailera holds global rights (ex-Greater China) to four Hengrui clinical assets, each targeting a distinct GLP-1-based mechanism or delivery route. Development approach leverages Hengrui's Chinese trial data to compress global Phase 2/3 timelines and inform patient stratification. Kailera's internal team adds global regulatory and commercial execution expertise.

## TAM logic

The global obesity drug market is projected to exceed $100B annually by 2030, driven by GLP-1 class growth (Goldman Sachs, various estimates). Kailera addresses the full treatment journey: patients starting therapy (injectable), patients wanting oral options (oral ribupatide, KAI-7535), and patients needing greater weight loss (KAI-4729 tri-agonist for higher BMI). (Source: kailera.com/about-us/)

## Validated by

Founded 2024 (launched October 2024) with $400M Series A (Atlas Venture, Bain Capital Life Sciences, RTW Investments; Hengrui received ~19.9% equity). October 2025: $600M Series B (led by Bain Capital Private Equity). April 2026: $625M IPO (NASDAQ: KLRA) — reported as the largest biotech IPO on record at launch, eclipsing Moderna's $600M 2018 debut. June 2026: ribupatide Phase 2 data presented at ADA 2026 (Hengrui and Kailera joint presentation). Q1 2026 financial results confirm all four programs in active clinical progression.

## Competes with

Novo Nordisk (semaglutide — Ozempic, Wegovy, oral semaglutide Rybelsus), Eli Lilly (tirzepatide — Mounjaro, Zepbound; retatrutide in pipeline), AstraZeneca (cotadutide), Pfizer (oral GLP-1 small molecules), Structure Therapeutics (oral GPCR agonist), Altimmune (pemvidutide), Carmot Therapeutics (acquired by Roche), Viking Therapeutics (VK2735).

## Complements

Hengrui Pharmaceuticals (source of assets and China clinical data), CROs for global Phase 3 execution, payers and PBMs for reimbursement pathway, commercial infrastructure partners for specialty pharmacy distribution.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Drug discovery and optimization (done by Hengrui in China), early clinical development (Phase 1/2 in China)
**Upstream of:** Regulatory approval (FDA/EMA), commercial launch, payer negotiations and formulary access

## Gaps

No proprietary discovery engine — fully dependent on Hengrui licensing. If Hengrui programs fail in global trials, there is no backup pipeline with independent IP. Competitive window is narrowing fast: Novo and Lilly are advancing next-generation assets (once-monthly, oral, and CNS-targeted formulations). Kailera's differentiation case (superior weight loss, better tolerability) must be proven in large global trials.
