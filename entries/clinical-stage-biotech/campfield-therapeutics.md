## One-liner

cAMPfield is a San Diego-based biotech advancing prifemilast, a once-daily oral PDE4B-selective inhibitor, into Phase 2b for ulcerative colitis and Phase 2 for Crohn's disease, with $180M Series A launched in 2026.

## Problem

IBD (Crohn's disease, ulcerative colitis) is a chronic immune-mediated condition affecting over 3 million Americans. Biologics (anti-TNF, anti-integrin, anti-IL-12/23) and JAK inhibitors dominate, but they require injection or infusion, carry immunosuppression risks, and see up to 40% primary non-response. Patients and physicians want effective, convenient oral options.

## Workaround

Biologic therapy is the current standard for moderate-to-severe IBD. Oral options (aminosalicylates for mild UC; ozanimod, upadacitinib for more advanced disease) exist but each carries tolerability or safety signals. PDE4 inhibition was validated in psoriasis (apremilast/Otezla) and is mechanistically relevant to IBD, but prior oral PDE4 inhibitors failed due to non-selective PDE4D inhibition causing nausea and CNS effects.

## Entry wedge

cAMPfield was purpose-built by Mountainfield Venture Partners around a single well-characterized in-licensed asset. Prifemilast is PDE4B-selective (the anti-inflammatory subtype), designed to minimize PDE4D-related dose-limiting adverse effects. The existing 700+ patient clinical history from Newsoara provides a de-risked package that compressed the normal discovery-to-clinic timeline. The founding team's direct product experience (Entyvio, Otezla, Zeposia) anchors the investor thesis.

## Method

Prifemilast (HPP737) inhibits PDE4B, an enzyme that degrades cAMP in immune cells. Elevated cAMP reduces production of pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-alpha, IL-23, IL-12) driving IBD. Selective PDE4B inhibition is intended to deliver the anti-inflammatory benefit of PDE4 blockade with reduced PDE4D-mediated side effects (nausea, headache). The compound is once-daily oral, taken as a pill.

## TAM logic

The North American IBD market was valued at $12.72B in 2025, with global totals considerably higher. AbbVie's Humira/Skyrizi and Takeda's Entyvio dominate; combined global IBD market is estimated above $20B. Oral agents that achieve endoscopic remission rates competitive with biologics would command significant market share given the preference for oral dosing among patients and prescribers.

## Validated by

$180M Series A led by Frazier Life Sciences with Deep Track Capital, Forbion, Abingworth, Venrock, Longitude Capital, Novo Holdings, RA Capital. 700+ patients dosed with prifemilast in prior clinical work by Newsoara. Founding team built Entyvio (Asit Parikh, Takeda), Otezla and Zeposia for IBD (Keith Usiskin, Celgene/BMS), and Prometheus Biosciences (Mark Stenhouse, COO).

## Competes with

AbbVie (Skyrizi, upadacitinib), J&J (Stelara, Tremfya), Takeda (Entyvio), Pfizer (Xeljanz). In the oral small molecule space: ozanimod (Bristol Myers Squibb), etrasimod (Pfizer), and upadacitinib (AbbVie). Emerging oral IL-23 inhibitors and other PDE4 programs in development from other biotechs.

## Complements

Newsoara Biopharma (originator, retains rights in certain territories). Diagnostics companies focused on IBD biomarker stratification (e.g., Prometheus Biosciences-derived technology). CROs experienced in IBD endoscopic trial design.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Newsoara Biopharma (licensor), prior clinical development work
**Upstream of:** Phase 3 trials, NDA submission, commercial launch, potential partnership with large pharma IBD franchise

## Gaps

Prifemilast's PDE4B selectivity is a mechanistic claim that will be tested in Phase 2, if tolerability issues persist at efficacious doses, the differentiation rationale collapses. IBD is extremely competitive: over 120 active US clinical trials. Small molecule oral efficacy in Crohn's (harder than UC) remains an unmet bar. Oral agents with best-in-class ambitions must still demonstrate endoscopic response rates matching biologics.
