## One-liner

Parabilis Medicines pioneered the Helicon platform, a class of stabilized alpha-helical peptides that can enter cells and bind flat intracellular protein surfaces, enabling drugs against historically undruggable targets like the Wnt/b-catenin pathway.

## Problem

An estimated 80% of biologically validated disease targets are intracellular proteins with flat interaction surfaces, inaccessible to antibodies (which cannot cross cell membranes) and unable to be bound by small molecules (which lack the interaction surface area). This leaves millions of patients without targeted therapies for diseases driven by transcription factor interactions, protein-protein interactions, and DNA-binding proteins inside cells.

## Workaround

Chemotherapy and non-targeted systemic therapies for cancers with Wnt/b-catenin or androgen receptor driver mutations. Partial targeting through upstream pathway inhibitors (e.g., BRAF/MEK inhibitors that hit downstream effectors, but not the driving node). PROTAC/molecular glue degraders cover some intracellular targets but still cannot engage flat surfaces or require ligandable pockets.

## Entry wedge

Zolucatetide (formerly FOG-001): first-in-class, first-ever direct inhibitor of the b-catenin:TCF protein-protein interaction, the key downstream node in the Wnt signaling pathway. Currently in Phase 1/2 trials in patients with desmoid tumors (rare solid tumor with near-universal Wnt pathway dependence), FAP, and Wnt-driven gastrointestinal cancers.

## Method

Helicons are stabilized alpha-helical peptides built from natural and non-canonical amino acids. The helical backbone mimics the structural motif that transcription factors use to interact with each other and with DNA. Key properties: (1) cell membrane permeability via engineered charge and lipophilicity; (2) high-affinity flat-surface binding across large interaction interfaces (like an intracellular antibody); (3) broad tunability via 1000+ side chain diversity; (4) protease and MHC evasion for stability and low immunogenicity. AI and physics-based computational tools accelerate design. Platform is applied to both inhibition (PPI blocking) and degradation (PROTAC-like targeted protein degradation).

## TAM logic

Wnt/b-catenin pathway mutations implicated in millions of cancer cases globally per year across colorectal, hepatocellular, gastric cancers, plus desmoid tumors. Parabilis positions the Helicon platform as applicable across any intracellular target, covering an estimated 80% of the druggable proteome currently without targeted agents. Regeneron $2.3B collaboration validates platform breadth. (Source: parabilismed.com/innovation/rd-focus/, parabilismed.com/innovation/about-helicons/)

IPO June 2026 (record-breaking $670M biotech IPO + $75M Regeneron concurrent private placement). ## Competes with

PROTAC/molecular glue degraders (Arvinas, C4 Therapeutics, Kymera): overlap on degradation approach but require ligandable pockets. Cyclic peptide approaches (Bicycle Therapeutics): extracellular targets. In Wnt specifically: no approved drug class; indirect path inhibitors like RNAi (Dicerna, now Novo) address upstream. Oncology competitors in desmoid tumors: nirogacestat (SpringWorks), sorafenib.

## Complements

CROs and academic cancer centers for Phase 1/2 execution, Regeneron as platform discovery partner, precision oncology genomics partners for patient stratification in Wnt-mutant tumors.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Structural biology target characterization, computational protein-protein interaction landscape mapping, genomic biomarker identification in cancer patients
**Upstream of:** Clinical phase 2/3 development, regulatory submission, commercial oncology launch

## Gaps

No approved product. Zolucatetide is first-in-class with no prior clinical validation of b-catenin:TCF inhibition in humans, both a moat and a risk. Oral/systemic PK of Helicons in patients is not yet fully characterized across indications. Platform has not yet yielded a second clinical candidate beyond zolucatetide.
