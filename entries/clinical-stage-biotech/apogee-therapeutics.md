## One-liner

Clinical-stage biotech developing half-life-extended monoclonal antibodies against validated I&I targets to enable quarterly or biannual dosing versus the biweekly standard of care.

## Problem

Current best-in-class biologics for atopic dermatitis and asthma (dupilumab, lebrikizumab, tezepelumab) require dosing every 2-4 weeks, creating injection burden and adherence problems for patients. Most targets in the Th2 pathway are already clinically validated, leaving the differentiation fight on PK and tolerability rather than mechanism novelty.

## Workaround

Patients use dupilumab (Dupixent, Sanofi/Regeneron) at Q2W or Q4W injection schedules. Physicians layer on additional agents for patients with comorbid asthma and eczema, increasing polypharmacy.

## Entry wedge

Zumilokibart (APG777): anti-IL-13 mAb engineered for extended half-life, targeting a dose frequency of Q3M-Q6M maintenance. Phase 2 APEX trial in moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis showed positive 16-week Part B data (May 2026). Also showing positive Ph1b signal in asthma.

## Method

FcRn-binding engineering on antibody Fc regions extends serum half-life from weeks to months. Apogee applies this across a portfolio of validated Th2 targets: IL-13 (zumilokibart), OX40L (APG990), IL-4Ra (APG808), TSLP (APG333), and rational combinations (APG279 = IL-13 + OX40L).

## TAM logic

Atopic dermatitis market alone is projected at $15B+ by 2030. Asthma biologic market adds another $8-10B. Eosinophilic esophagitis is smaller but high unmet need. Apogee is positioning extended dosing as the durable market share capture strategy across multiple large I&I indications. (Source: apogeetherapeutics.com/science)

## Validated by

IPO July 2023 (NASDAQ: APGE), raising ~$345M. March 2024 secondary offering $403M. March 2026 follow-on offering $403M (priced at $350M + overallotment). May 2026: $1.3B strategic financing from Blackstone Life Sciences to fund Phase 3 and commercialization of zumilokibart. Positive Phase 2 APEX Part A (52-week data) and Part B (16-week data) presented at AAD, EADV, RAD 2025-2026 congresses. Phase 1b data in asthma positive (ATS 2026). Multiple programs (APG808, APG990) in Phase 1.

## Competes with

Sanofi/Regeneron (Dupixent, dupilumab), Eli Lilly (lebrikizumab), AstraZeneca (tezepelumab), Pfizer (abrocitinib, JAK inhibitor rather than biologic), LEO Pharma. In extended half-life specifically: Hanall Biopharma's efineptakin alfa, Aiolos Bio (GlaxoSmithKline-acquired anti-TSLP).

## Complements

CROs for Phase 3 execution, contract manufacturers for antibody production, patient advocacy organizations in atopic dermatitis and asthma communities.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Target biology discovery, antibody engineering platforms, FcRn PK research
**Upstream of:** Commercial launch, payer reimbursement, patient support programs

## Gaps

No approved products. Phase 3 data and regulatory approval still required for zumilokibart. Head-to-head superiority vs. dupilumab not yet demonstrated in a registrational trial. Extended half-life benefit (Q3M vs. Q2W) requires patients to value convenience highly enough to switch from an established therapy with strong safety data.

## Notes

Founded in 2022 with $169M (Series A + B) from Fairmount, Venrock, Deep Track, RTW. Listed NASDAQ July 2023. Ticker: APGE. The $1.3B Blackstone deal (May 2026) is a royalty plus debt financing structure rather than dilutive equity, a significant validation of zumilokibart's commercial potential. Mathai Mammen (former J&J Global Head of R&D) is CEO.
