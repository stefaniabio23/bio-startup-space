## One-liner

Develops drugs targeting the root causes of cardiovascular diseases that have no approved treatments, built by the team that sold MyoKardia to Bristol Myers Squibb for $13 billion.

## Problem

Cardiovascular disease remains the leading cause of death globally, yet several mechanistically distinct cardiac conditions, including genetic dilated cardiomyopathy, calcific aortic valve stenosis, and acute severe hypertension with target organ damage, have no approved therapies.

## Workaround

Symptom management with heart failure medications (beta blockers, ACE inhibitors, diuretics), surgical interventions (valve replacement, LVAD implantation), and general antihypertensives used off-label in acute hypertension, none of which address the underlying disease mechanism.

## Entry wedge

Kardigan was founded in January 2025 by Tassos Gianakakos (former MyoKardia CEO) and Jay Edelberg (former MyoKardia CMO), who led MyoKardia through its development of mavacamten (Camzyos) and the $13B acquisition by BMS. The founding team used credibility and KOL relationships from the MyoKardia era to immediately raise a $300M Series A (January 2025), license three clinical-stage cardiovascular assets from external partners, initiate three Phase 2b/3 trials within one year of founding, and acquire Prolaio (a cardiac clinical intelligence company, March 2025). A further $254M Series B closed in 2025. Kardigan priced an upsized IPO on Nasdaq (ticker: KARD) on June 17, 2026, selling 25 million shares at $16.00/share for $400M gross proceeds (above the original $14-16 range and $327-373M target), with a $60M overallotment option.

## Method

Three late-stage clinical programs, all in-licensed:

1. **Danicamtiv** (lead): Oral cardiac myosin activator (positive inotrope) for genetic dilated cardiomyopathy (DCM) caused by MYH7 or TTN gene variants. In Phase 2b/3 KINSHIP-DCM trial vs. placebo; results expected 2027. Addresses a DCM subpopulation with no approved disease-modifying treatment.

2. **Tonlamarsen**: Subcutaneous antisense oligonucleotide (ASO) targeting angiotensinogen mRNA to lower blood pressure at the source. In Phase 2b for acute severe hypertension with target organ damage. Novel mechanism: upstream angiotensin suppression rather than receptor blockade.

3. **Ataciguat**: Oral soluble guanylate cyclase (sGC) activator for calcific aortic valve stenosis (CAVS). In Phase 2b. No approved medical therapy exists for CAVS; standard of care is surgical or TAVR valve replacement.

Kardigan acquired Prolaio (March 2025), a cardiovascular clinical intelligence company co-founded by Gianakakos, that builds real-world data infrastructure for cardiac disease monitoring and hospital-to-home transition care for hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM) patients. Prolaio provides Kardigan with a "sharper trials" data layer for patient selection and trial execution, consistent with Gianakakos's stated intent to integrate digital health into the cardio strategy.

## TAM logic

Cardiovascular disease is the largest cause of global mortality and a $50B+ annual drug market. The specific TAM for the three Kardigan indications is smaller, genetic DCM affects approximately 1 in 2,500 people; CAVS prevalence increases steeply with age; acute severe hypertension is a common emergency presentation. Combined, these orphan-adjacent cardiovascular markets support premium pricing and relatively small trial sizes.

## Competes with

BMS (mavacamten / Camzyos for HCM, same cardiac myosin biology; danicamtiv targets DCM which is the opposite mechanism, activator not inhibitor), Rocket Pharmaceuticals and other gene therapy companies targeting genetic cardiomyopathy, Alnylam (ASO/siRNA platform could address similar angiotensinogen targets), Edwards Lifesciences and Medtronic (TAVR devices for CAVS, Kardigan's ataciguat would compete with procedural intervention if approved).

## Complements

Prolaio (acquired, now internal): digital cardiovascular intelligence for trial optimization and real-world monitoring. Academic cardiac centers (Cleveland Clinic, Mayo Clinic Cardiovascular) as trial sites and KOL networks. Genetic testing companies (Invitae, GeneDx) for identifying MYH7/TTN mutation carriers eligible for KINSHIP-DCM.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Academic cardiovascular science (mechanism validation), genetic testing (patient identification for DCM trial), BMS and other licensors (source of drug assets)
**Upstream of:** Cardiologists and heart failure specialists (prescribers), hospital systems and specialty pharmacies (distribution), payers (coverage decisions)

## Gaps

All three programs are in-licensed rather than internally discovered, Kardigan is a development and execution company, not a discovery engine. If pivotal trials fail, there is no internal pipeline to fall back on. The DCM population defined by MYH7/TTN mutations is relatively small, which constrains peak revenue potential for danicamtiv even on approval. Prolaio's digital health integration is described strategically but has not yet been validated as a trial acceleration tool in published outcomes. Key value-inflection readouts are on a 2027 data calendar, so the public valuation rests on late-stage risk across three simultaneous programs that have not yet read out.

## Notes

Tassos Gianakakos is simultaneously CEO of both Kardigan and Prolaio (pre-acquisition), an unusual arrangement that signals the companies were designed as an integrated unit from the start. The MyoKardia alumni network is a genuine advantage: the team knows how to run cardiac trials, build cardiomyopathy patient registries, and navigate FDA interactions for novel cardiac mechanisms. Kardigan's strategy is explicitly to combine drug development with digital health infrastructure (via Prolaio) to run sharper, faster trials, a model worth watching as a template for data-enriched cardiovascular drug development.
