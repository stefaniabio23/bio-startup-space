## One-liner

Uses longitudinal human aging cohorts and ML to identify targets for age-related diseases and develop therapeutics.

## Problem

Static biology snapshots miss the dynamic drivers of aging-linked disease progression.

## Workaround

Epidemiology, genetic association studies, animal aging models, biomarker discovery, conventional drug development.

## Entry wedge

BioAge built a proprietary longitudinal cohort platform before developing any drug. The founding substrate was multi-decade human aging datasets, serial multi-omics and functional assessments on healthy individuals tracked over time, combined with in-house ML to mine aging biology. This data asset came first; therapeutics followed from it. The NLRP3 program (BGE-102) is the first drug to emerge from that pipeline and entered Phase 1 clinical trials.

## Method

ML analysis of longitudinal multi-omics from proprietary human aging cohorts to identify molecular drivers of cardiometabolic disease, followed by in-house medicinal chemistry to develop small molecule therapeutics against those targets.

## TAM logic

BioAge targets metabolic diseases driven by aging, they state approximately 2 billion people have cardiometabolic disease and 988 million live with obesity globally. Their lead programs address cardiovascular risk (BGE-102, NLRP3 inhibitor) and obesity (APJ agonist). The $34.5B TAM figure in frontmatter reflects the clinical-stage cardiometabolic drug market. (Source: bioagelabs.com/platform)

## Validated by

IPO Sept 2024 ($198M raised). Upsized follow-on public offering of $132.3M completed early 2026. BGE-102 (NLRP3 inhibitor) reported positive Phase 1 data in April 2026, showing potential best-in-class hsCRP reductions with a favorable tolerability profile. Phase 2 dose-ranging proof-of-concept trial in cardiovascular risk planned to initiate mid-2026 with data anticipated by year end. Three peer-reviewed preprints on NLRP3 inhibitor chemistry (BioRxiv 2024-2025, Bioorganic & Medicinal Chemistry Letters 2024).

## Competes with

Other aging-biology biotechs pursuing cardiometabolic indications: Calico (Google), Unity Biotechnology (senolytic), Alkahest (plasma-derived). Indirectly competes with GLP-1 incumbents (Novo Nordisk, Eli Lilly) in obesity, where their APJ agonist is positioned as an add-on to incretin therapy rather than a replacement.

## Complements

GLP-1 manufacturers (Novo Nordisk, Lilly): APJ agonist is explicitly framed as a combination therapy on an "incretin background." Contract research organizations running Phase 1/2 metabolic disease trials. Aging research consortia supplying longitudinal cohort data.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Longitudinal cohort data, aging biology research
**Upstream of:** Clinical trials, drug development programs

## Gaps

Longitudinal cohort methodology is an interesting precedent; proving that intervening on aging pathways improves disease outcomes is still the hard problem. Platform generates targets but reproducibility of aging biomarker insights across diverse populations is unproven at scale. No marketed product yet; commercial execution risk is high.
