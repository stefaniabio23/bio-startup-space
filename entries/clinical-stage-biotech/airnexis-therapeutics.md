## One-liner

AirNexis is a respiratory biotech advancing a dual PDE3/4 inhibitor (AN01) in Phase 2 for COPD, launched with $200M Series A in January 2026 on the back of an in-license from China's Haisco Pharmaceutical Group.

## Problem

COPD affects over 300 million people globally and remains undertreated despite a crowded inhaled therapy market. Existing bronchodilators and anti-inflammatory agents address airflow limitation or inflammation separately; no widely available single agent reliably targets both simultaneously via oral or inhalation dosing beyond roflumilast (PDE4-only, oral, systemic side-effect burden).

## Workaround

Current standard of care uses triple inhaled therapy (LABA/LAMA/ICS) and, for some patients, the PDE4 inhibitor roflumilast. The dual PDE3/4 mechanism (combined bronchodilation + anti-inflammatory) via inhaled route has been validated by ensifentrine (Merck, approved 2024) but gaps remain in delivering it via suspension or powder formats suitable for broader patient populations.

## Entry wedge

Founded by Frazier Life Sciences as a purpose-built company around a single in-licensed asset with an existing Phase 2 data package from China. The entry wedge is capital efficiency: Haisco's Phase 1/2 data de-risked the asset enough to attract $200M in a heavily oversubscribed round. AirNexis trades equity (19.9% to Haisco) and milestone obligations ($955M biobucks) for accelerated access to a clinical-stage compound without the cost of early discovery.

## Method

AN01 (HSK39004) is a dual PDE3/4 inhibitor delivered via inhalation (suspension and powder forms). It promotes bronchodilation through PDE3 inhibition (relaxes airway smooth muscle) and reduces airway inflammation through PDE4 inhibition, potentially delivering synergistic benefit over single-mechanism agents. AirNexis is running Phase 2 trials in ex-China territories, with Haisco retaining rights in Greater China.

## TAM logic

Global COPD treatment market estimated at ~$22.9B in 2026 (Business Research Company) growing at ~5.4% CAGR. AirNexis is targeting maintenance therapy for moderate-to-severe COPD. Even a modest share of this market, combined with regulatory milestones, justifies the $200M launch capital given precedent valuations (ensifentrine launched into same segment).

## Validated by

$200M Series A closed January 2026, oversubscribed, led by Frazier Life Sciences with OrbiMed, SR One, Goldman Sachs Life Sciences, Longitude Capital, Enavate Sciences. Haisco received $40M upfront + 19.9% equity stake + up to $955M in milestones and low double-digit royalties, implying meaningful third-party validation of the asset.

## Competes with

Ensifentrine (Verona Pharma/Merck, approved 2024), the closest analog as an inhaled dual PDE3/4 inhibitor already on market. Roflumilast (AstraZeneca), oral PDE4-only. Triple inhaled therapy (LABA/LAMA/ICS) regimens from AstraZeneca, GSK, Boehringer Ingelheim, Novartis.

## Complements

Chinese pharma partners (Haisco retains Greater China rights). Contract research organizations running respiratory Phase 2/3 trials. Potential co-development partners among larger respiratory pharma companies who might license or acquire the asset post-Phase 2 readout.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Haisco Pharmaceutical Group (originator), Chinese Phase 1/2 clinical data
**Upstream of:** Phase 3 trials, NDA submission, commercial launch, potential pharma partnership or acquisition

## Gaps

AirNexis was founded simultaneously with the announcement, there is no track record of clinical execution. The competitive moat is thin given ensifentrine is already on market. Success depends heavily on differentiation (inhalation route, safety profile, convenience vs. ensifentrine) that Phase 2 data will need to establish. Haisco's equity stake and milestone structure creates structural overhang on a future M&A exit.
