## One-liner

Vertically integrated AI drug discovery company using "polymathic AI" and in-house wet-lab biology to develop precision-medicine treatments for underserved brain diseases, starting with brain tumours.

## Problem

Brain-tumour research is chronically underfunded (roughly 1% of UK cancer research spend, 2002-2021) and clinical outcomes remain poor. The blood-brain barrier and tumour heterogeneity have defeated most conventional discovery approaches.

## Workaround

Traditional pharma neuro-oncology pipelines, academic labs, standard target-discovery workflows, repurposing of existing oncology assets that rarely cross into the brain effectively.

## Entry wedge

Underserved brain-tumour biology plus vertically integrated AI. CoSyne works backwards from the clinic rather than from a technology in search of a problem, pairing a London computational lab with a Cambridge experimental lab to pursue targets that larger players consider too hard.

## Method

Combines single-cell sequencing, falling genomic costs, and GPU computing with what the company calls polymathic AI, applied by scientists to generate qualitatively better hypotheses about brain-disease biology and candidate therapeutics, then tested in its own wet lab.

## TAM logic

The global oncology therapeutics market is worth tens of billions, and CNS/brain-tumour indications are a high-unmet-need slice with premium pricing potential for any approved precision therapy. As an own-pipeline biotech, value accrues through program milestones, partnerships, or acquisition rather than near-term revenue. (Source: cosyne.com)

## Validated by

Approximately $8.81M (about £6M) equity round closing May 2022, led by Backed VC with Jonathan Milner (founder of Abcam, acquired for nearly $6B), LocalGlobe/Phoenix Court, and Amino Collective. Strategic advisors across New York, Seattle, and California. Note: public validation is thin, no disclosed clinical-stage assets.

## Competes with

Recursion, Insilico Medicine, Isomorphic Labs, and other AI drug-discovery platforms, plus traditional neuro-oncology pipelines at large pharma.

## Complements

CROs for eventual trials, sequencing and reagent suppliers, academic neuro-oncology collaborators, potential pharma partners for later-stage development.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Genomic and single-cell data, disease biology, compute
**Upstream of:** Preclinical candidates, IND-enabling studies, clinical trials

## Gaps

Very thin public information. Despite the "clinical-stage" folder placement, CoSyne appears to be preclinical/discovery-stage with no disclosed clinical assets. Small seed-scale funding relative to well-capitalised AI-biotech peers. The polymathic-AI claim is unvalidated externally. Brain-tumour discovery carries high scientific and translational risk.
