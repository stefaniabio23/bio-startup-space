## One-liner

Develops clinical-stage medicines for autoimmune and inflammatory diseases using precision immunology.

## Problem

Autoimmune and inflammatory diseases still have incomplete response rates, toxicity, and poor patient stratification.

## Workaround

Incumbent immunology drugs, broad pathway inhibition, biologics, steroids, trial-and-error prescribing.

## Entry wedge

Founded in 2021 with a focus on upstream innate immune signaling nodes. First program: RIPK2 inhibitor (OD-001) for ulcerative colitis and Crohn's disease, developed entirely internally. IPO on NASDAQ (ticker: ODTX) in May 2026 at $18/share raised ~$304M.

## Method

Medicinal and computational chemistry combined with structural biology and pharmacology to identify small molecules and protein therapeutics that block upstream innate immune activation nodes, targeting the signal before pathologic inflammation initiates rather than suppressing downstream cytokines after the fact.

## TAM logic

Autoimmune and inflammatory disease market estimated at $168.6B. Odyssey argues that upstream innate targeting can address multiple large patient populations across diseases (IBD, lupus, atopic dermatitis, asthma, COPD, HS) simultaneously, because the innate cascade is shared across indications (odysseytx.com, pipeline page).

## Validated by

IPO in May 2026 on NASDAQ Capital Market (ODTX, $18/share). RIPK2 program (OD-001) advancing through clinical milestones as of Q1 2026 financial update. Six programs across target discovery through Phase 2-ready stages.

## Competes with

AbbVie (Skyrizi, Rinvoq), Pfizer (Cibinqo), Sanofi/Regeneron (Dupixent), Eli Lilly (lebrikizumab), AstraZeneca in IBD/lupus/AD. Clinical-stage precision immunology biotechs: Protagonist Therapeutics, Acelyrin, Alumis, Tyra Biosciences.

## Complements

CROs for trial execution. Biomarker companies for patient stratification (Tempus, Foundation Medicine). Latent-state modeling tools that could predict responder subgroups during Phase 2.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Target identification research, structural biology, computational chemistry platforms, genomics (GWAS, human genetics)
**Upstream of:** Clinical trial infrastructure, regulatory approval, commercialization, payers and prescribers

## Gaps

Less obviously frontier-AI than Isomorphic/Chai/Latent; success depends on disease biology, trial design, biomarkers, dosing, and patient population execution. A clinical-stage biotech like Odyssey is exactly the kind of company that would benefit from latent-state inference tools during trials.

## Notes

Six pipeline programs across two modalities (small molecules and protein therapeutics). Focus on upstream innate immune responses differentiates from most current standard-of-care biologics, which suppress downstream cytokines. Founder/CEO Gary D. Glick, PhD leads experienced drug hunter team.
