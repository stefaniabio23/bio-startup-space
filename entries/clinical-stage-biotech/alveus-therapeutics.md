## One-liner

Alveus is a Philadelphia/Copenhagen obesity biotech advancing ALV-100, a GIPR antagonist / GLP-1R agonist fusion protein, in Phase 2, and ALV-200, a selective AMYR3 amylin agonist, in IND-enabling studies, backed by $197M Series A.

## Problem

Current GLP-1R agonists (semaglutide) and dual GLP-1/GIP agonists (tirzepatide) deliver significant weight loss but come with tolerability issues (nausea, vomiting, lean mass loss) and require weekly injection. A fraction of patients cannot tolerate effective doses. More durable weight loss with maintained lean body mass and improved tolerability remains unmet.

## Workaround

First-generation GLP-1 therapies (Ozempic, Wegovy) and dual agonist tirzepatide (Mounjaro, Zepbound) are the current treatment standard. Semaglutide plus cagrilintide (amylin analog, Novo Nordisk) is in Phase 3. Amycretin (Novo) combines GLP-1 and amylin in one molecule. The space is crowded with next-generation approaches but no clearly dominant follow-on has emerged.

## Entry wedge

Alveus entered with a differentiated biology thesis: ALV-100 combines GIP receptor antagonism (rather than agonism used by tirzepatide) with GLP-1 receptor agonism in a fusion protein format. GIP antagonism is the mechanism in Pfizer's MariTide (AMG133), and Alveus is described as "MariTide-like" by the press. The second leg, ALV-200, targets AMYR3 selectively (avoiding CTR-driven GI side effects of prior amylin analogs), drawing on institutional Novo Nordisk / Eli Lilly expertise embedded in the founding team.

## Method

ALV-100 is a bispecific fusion protein: GLP-1R agonism drives incretin-mediated weight loss (insulin secretion, appetite suppression) while GIPR antagonism removes GIP's pro-lipogenic effects and potentially enhances the GLP-1 signal. ALV-200 is a peptide agonist selective for amylin receptor 3 (AMYR3) over the calcitonin receptor (CTR); AMYR3 is proposed to mediate beneficial amylin effects (lean mass preservation, satiety) while CTR activation is linked to nausea and aversion.

## TAM logic

The global obesity drug market is projected to exceed $100B by the mid-2030s. Alveus targets both obesity and metabolic disease complications. Even a single approved program in this space commands multi-billion-dollar peak sales given the scale of the obesity epidemic (1 billion affected globally per WHO estimates).

## Competes with

Novo Nordisk (semaglutide, cagrilintide, amycretin). Eli Lilly (tirzepatide, retatrutide). Pfizer (MariTide/AMG133, GIPR antagonist / GLP-1R agonist, the closest structural analog to ALV-100). Amgen (MariTide). Kailera Therapeutics (dual GLP-1/GIP agonist, different GIPR mechanism). Structure Therapeutics, Terns Pharmaceuticals (oral GLP-1). Dozens of other next-gen obesity biotechs funded 2024-2026.

## Complements

Potential partnership or licensing deal with large pharma wanting a differentiated obesity asset to complement an existing portfolio. CROs specializing in obesity Phase 2 trial design (dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry endpoints for lean mass, long-duration weight loss assessments).

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Human genetic target validation, amylin biology research, Novo Nordisk / Lilly scientific heritage
**Upstream of:** Phase 3 obesity trials, NDA submission, commercial obesity drug launch, partnership with large pharma obesity franchises

## Gaps

ALV-100 occupies the same mechanistic niche as Pfizer's MariTide, which is further along and backed by a large-pharma commercial infrastructure. Phase 2 data will determine whether ALV-100 is meaningfully differentiated from MariTide on efficacy or tolerability. The obesity space has attracted unprecedented capital; crowding creates risk of undifferentiated outcomes. ALV-200's AMYR3 selectivity claim is preclinical and has not been validated in humans. Founded 2024 with no prior public clinical data at launch.
