## One-liner

Hesperos builds multi-organ Human-on-a-Chip microphysiological systems with functional electrical and mechanical readouts, offering animal-free drug safety and efficacy testing as a contract service.

## Problem

Animal models poorly predict human drug responses, driving high clinical-trial failure rates and slow, costly development. Standard cell assays miss the coupled, functional behaviour of interacting human organs.

## Workaround

Drug developers rely on animal studies and simple 2D cell cultures despite known translational gaps. Some use single-organ chips or organoids that lack functional, multi-organ readouts and inter-organ signalling.

## Entry wedge

Sell contract compound testing services on the Human-on-a-Chip platform to pharma, biotech, cosmetic, and food companies, anchored by the credibility of being first to support an IND with organ-on-chip efficacy data.

## Method

Human primary and stem-cell-derived cells are cultured in serum-free defined systems as interconnected organ modules (for example neuromuscular junction, cardiac, liver) on a chip. Functional responses are measured non-invasively via electrical (electrophysiology) and mechanical (force) readouts under recirculating flow, producing safety and efficacy data.

## Validated by

Efficacy data from a Hesperos system was included in an IND filed with Sanofi in December 2020 for a rare autoimmune disease, the first time microphysiological-system data supported authorization of a clinical trial. Programs supported by the platform have since advanced into Phase 2 and Phase 3.

## Competes with

Other organ-on-chip and microphysiological-system companies such as Emulate, CN Bio, Mimetas, and TissUse, plus traditional animal-model CROs and organoid platforms.

## Complements

Pharma preclinical pipelines, FDA New Approach Methodologies (NAMs) initiatives, the FDA Modernization Act 2.0 shift away from mandatory animal testing, and digital-twin modelling of disease.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** cell sourcing and reagents, microfabrication and chip manufacturing, drug-discovery hit and lead generation.

**Upstream of:** IND filings, clinical trials, and regulatory decision-making.

## Gaps

Funding stage and total raised are not publicly disclosed; the company is grant-heavy (NIH SBIR) with a 2023 strategic investment from Btomorrow Ventures (British American Tobacco). Throughput, standardisation, and broad regulatory codification of MPS data remain open constraints.

## Notes

Founded 2015 by Michael L. Shuler (President) and James J. Hickman (Chief Scientist), spun out of University of Central Florida and the NIH/DARPA MPS program. Serves as a contract research organization rather than an owned-pipeline drug developer. Relevant to substrate E.6 (organoids/tissue constructs) and D.2 (diagnostic/disease model).
