## One-liner

Locus Biosciences engineers CRISPR-Cas3-enhanced bacteriophage cocktails (crPhage) that lyse a target bacterium while a Cas3 payload irreversibly degrades its DNA, aimed at drug-resistant infections.

## Problem

Multidrug-resistant bacteria, led by E. coli in urinary tract infections, are outrunning conventional antibiotics, and broad-spectrum drugs wipe out the protective microbiome while breeding further resistance. There is no approved therapy that kills a resistant pathogen with species-level precision.

## Workaround

Clinicians escalate to last-line antibiotics such as carbapenems or use repeated courses, accepting collateral microbiome damage, rising resistance, and recurrent infection. Compassionate-use natural phage exists but is unengineered and inconsistent.

## Entry wedge

Uncomplicated urinary tract infection caused by E. coli, a large, well-defined indication with a clear resistance problem and a measurable clinical endpoint, addressed by lead candidate LBP-EC01, a cocktail of six engineered phages.

## Method

Natural phages that infect E. coli are armed with a CRISPR-Cas3 construct so that, on infection, the bacterium's own DNA is shredded in addition to phage-mediated lysis. Candidates are selected and combined into cocktails using an in-house high-throughput discovery platform to broaden host range and limit resistance.

## Validated by

Positive Part 1 results from the Phase 2 ELIMINATE trial were published in The Lancet Infectious Diseases (2024), meeting primary and secondary objectives, after an earlier controlled Phase 1b safety study. Development is co-funded by BARDA under a program valued up to roughly $144M.

## Competes with

Standard-of-care and last-line antibiotics for resistant UTI, and other anti-infective phage developers such as Armata Pharmaceuticals, BiomX, and Adaptive Phage Therapeutics/other CRISPR-antimicrobial efforts like Eligo Bioscience.

## Complements

Diagnostic pathogen-identification workflows, antimicrobial stewardship programs, and microbiome-preserving care pathways that need a narrow-spectrum agent instead of a broad antibiotic.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** foundational CRISPR-Cas3 IP licensed from NC State and Duke, phage discovery and engineering platforms (including the acquired EpiBiome high-throughput capability), and government non-dilutive funders (BARDA, CARB-X, NIH).

**Upstream of:** treating providers, hospital pharmacies, and payers who would prescribe and reimburse an approved anti-infective.

## Gaps

Registrational Part 2 efficacy and durability data are still being generated, the manufacturing and regulatory path for a living multi-phage drug is unproven at commercial scale, and the company remains private and capital-dependent on continued BARDA support.

## Notes

Founded 2015 as an NC State spin-out, based in Morrisville, North Carolina; founder Paul Garofolo. Disclosed venture equity totals about $54M (2017 Series A ~$19M led by Artis Ventures; 2022 Series B ~$35M with Artis, Viking Global, and Johnson & Johnson Development Corporation). Substantial non-dilutive funding on top: BARDA up to ~$77M within a ~$144M program, CARB-X ~$12.5M, and NIH ~$3.3M. The 2019 Janssen (Johnson & Johnson) collaboration for respiratory-tract phage programs, worth up to ~$818M in milestones with $20M upfront, was later ended and rights returned to Locus. total_funding_usd_m reflects disclosed venture equity only; grant funding is additive.
