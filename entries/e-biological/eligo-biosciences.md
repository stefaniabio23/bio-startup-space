## One-liner

Eligo Bioscience edits the genomes of specific bacteria directly inside the body using phage-derived particles that deliver CRISPR base-editor payloads, turning commensal microbes into localized biologic factories or eliminating disease-driving strains.

## Problem

Many diseases are driven by specific bacterial strains in the gut or skin, but broad-spectrum antibiotics and probiotics cannot selectively remove or reprogram one strain without disturbing the whole microbiome. There is no precision tool to change what resident bacteria do inside a living host.

## Workaround

Current practice relies on antibiotics that indiscriminately kill both harmful and beneficial bacteria, probiotics that add strains without controlling existing ones, and systemic biologics that act body-wide rather than at the site of dysregulation.

## Entry wedge

The lead immuno-dermatology program is a first-generation CRISPR-based topical therapeutic for moderate-to-severe acne vulgaris, targeting Cutibacterium acnes strains that express pro-inflammatory molecules. Topical skin delivery narrows the delivery and regulatory challenge relative to systemic gut editing.

## Method

Engineered phage-derived particles (Eligobiotics) deliver a CRISPR base editor into target bacteria, rewriting single DNA bases without the double-strand cut that would kill the cell. This preserves microbiome balance while either silencing a harmful gene or converting a commensal into a bioreactor expressing a therapeutic payload in situ.

## Validated by

In 2024 the company published in-situ targeted base editing of E. coli and Klebsiella in the mouse gut in Nature, with edited bacteria stably maintained for at least 42 days. A prior GSK partnership (2021) and a 2025 French government France 2030 grant fund the acne and immuno-dermatology programs.

## Competes with

Snipr Biome (CRISPR-guided precision antibacterials), Locus Biosciences (CRISPR-Cas3 phage therapy), and broadly antibiotic and biologic dermatology incumbents for the acne indication.

## Complements

Microbial CDMOs such as Biose Industries for fermentation and bioproduction, phage-discovery platforms, and pharma partners like GSK that provide clinical development and commercialization reach.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** phage biology, CRISPR base-editing science, microbiome strain characterization, and microbial fermentation manufacturing.

**Upstream of:** clinical-stage living therapeutics, dermatology and gastroenterology drug pipelines, and pharma licensing partners.

## Gaps

No human clinical efficacy data yet; lead programs remain preclinical. Manufacturing of phage-derived particles at clinical scale is unproven, and no regulatory precedent exists for an in-vivo bacterial gene-editing therapeutic.

## Notes

Founded 2014 in Paris by Xavier Duportet, David Bikard, Timothy Lu, and Luciano Marraffini. Roughly 47 employees as of 2026. Total disclosed funding around 52.3M USD across seed, a 2017 20M Series A (Seventure, Khosla Ventures), and a Series B expanded to about 35M, plus a 2025 5M non-dilutive France 2030 grant. The EB005 acne designation appears in earlier program references; the current company framing describes it as the lead CRISPR-based topical acne therapeutic.
