## One-liner

Capstan Therapeutics builds targeted lipid nanoparticles that deliver CAR mRNA to T cells inside the body, making CAR-T cells in vivo to treat B-cell-driven autoimmune disease.

## Problem

Conventional CAR-T therapy requires extracting a patient's T cells, engineering them in a lab, and reinfusing them, a slow, costly, individualized manufacturing process with harsh preconditioning. That complexity keeps CAR-T largely confined to oncology and out of reach for the far larger autoimmune population.

## Workaround

Autoimmune patients today rely on broad immunosuppressants and antibody drugs that dampen B-cell activity without resetting the immune system, requiring chronic dosing. Ex-vivo CAR-T for autoimmune disease exists in trials but carries the manufacturing burden and cost of the oncology model.

## Entry wedge

Capstan's lead candidate CPTX2309 is an anti-CD19 in-vivo CAR-T for B-cell-mediated autoimmune diseases, a first indication where transient B-cell depletion can reset the immune system and where the tLNP approach sidesteps ex-vivo manufacturing.

## Method

A CD8-targeted lipid nanoparticle carries mRNA encoding an anti-CD19 CAR to cytotoxic T cells in the bloodstream. The T cells transiently express the CAR, kill CD19-positive B cells, and the mRNA degrades, giving a self-limiting dose without permanent genomic edits or preconditioning chemotherapy.

## Validated by

The technology derives from Penn work by collaborators of Carl June and Drew Weissman. Capstan dosed the first patient in a Phase 1 trial of CPTX2309 in 2025. AbbVie validated the platform commercially by agreeing to acquire Capstan for up to $2.1 billion in June 2025.

## Competes with

Other in-vivo CAR-T and cell-reprogramming players such as Umoja Biopharma, Interius BioTherapeutics, and EsoBiotec, plus ex-vivo autoimmune CAR-T programs (Cabaletta Bio, Kyverna) and established B-cell-depleting antibody therapies.

## Complements

mRNA and LNP suppliers, autoimmune disease diagnostics, and AbbVie's existing immunology franchise, which gains an in-vivo cell-engineering platform to pair with its antibody and small-molecule portfolio.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** mRNA payload design, lipid nanoparticle chemistry, CAR construct IP, and academic immunology research at Penn.
**Upstream of:** clinical trials, regulatory approval, and delivery of an off-the-shelf in-vivo CAR-T therapeutic to autoimmune patients.

## Gaps

Long-term safety and durability of in-vivo CAR-T in autoimmune patients remain unproven. Manufacturing consistency of targeted LNPs at scale, off-target cell delivery, and the durability of a transient mRNA-driven B-cell reset are open clinical questions.

## Notes

Founded in 2021 as a San Diego spinout building on Penn research, Capstan raised roughly $340M across seed, Series A, and a $175M oversubscribed Series B (March 2024) backed by RA Capital, J&J, Bayer, BMS, Novartis, Pfizer, Lilly, and others. AbbVie's up-to-$2.1B acquisition, announced June 30, 2025, folds the tLNP platform and CPTX2309 into AbbVie's immunology pipeline. Preclinical programs include a BCMA target (oncology and autoimmune) and a fibroblast target for fibrotic disease.
