## One-liner

Biodesign company that computationally designs animal-free proteins (collagen, elastin, keratin) and makes them by microbial precision fermentation as ingredients for beauty, personal care, and food.

## Problem

Collagen, elastin, and keratin used in cosmetics and nutrition are extracted from animal tissue (bovine, marine, porcine), which brings supply variability, contamination and ethical concerns, sequence heterogeneity, and no way to design a protein for a specific performance target. Brands going vegan or clean-label have no drop-in animal-free equivalent that performs.

## Workaround

Animal-derived collagen and gelatin from slaughterhouse and fishery byproducts, plant-protein substitutes that do not match collagen or elastin biology, and synthetic peptide mimetics that approximate rather than reproduce the human protein.

## Entry wedge

Started with animal-free collagen for the beauty and personal-care market, where willingness to pay is high, regulatory burden is light relative to therapeutics, and the vegan and clean-beauty trend creates pull. Launched biomimetic human elastin (Elastapure) and human type XXI collagen (HumaColl 21) as differentiated ingredients brands cannot source elsewhere, then extended into food and beverage with GRAS-cleared collagen.

## Method

The Biodesigner AI platform designs protein sequences (human-identical or biomimetic variants of collagen, elastin, keratin) tuned for a functional target, then expresses them in engineered microbial hosts by precision fermentation. The fermented protein is purified into a cosmetic-grade or food-grade polypeptide ingredient. The loop couples computational sequence design to fermentation and downstream formulation performance data.

## Validated by

Total funding of about $137M, with a 2025 equity round co-led by Starlight Ventures and iSelect Fund; earlier a $91.3M Series B. PrimaColl received an FDA "no questions" response to its GRAS notification, the first biotech collagen to reach that milestone, and won a 2026 Cosmetics and Toiletries Allē Award. Commercial ingredient lines (Elastapure, HumaColl 21, Collume, Collagen sold into named consumer brands).

## Competes with

Other precision-fermentation and animal-free protein ingredient makers (Modern Meadow / Brava, Jellatech, Provenance Bio, Cambrium on designer collagen; Perfect Day and Change Foods on fermentation-derived proteins more broadly) and incumbent animal-derived collagen and gelatin suppliers (Rousselot, Gelita, Nitta Gelatin) on price.

## Complements

Consumer beauty, personal-care, wellness, and food brands that formulate the ingredients into finished products; contract fermentation and biomanufacturing capacity; regulatory and GRAS consultants; and synthetic-biology strain and enzyme suppliers upstream.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Protein target selection, microbial strain engineering, and fermentation feedstock supply.
**Upstream of:** Brand formulation, finished cosmetic and food products, and retail distribution.

## Gaps

Cost parity with cheap animal-derived collagen at scale is the persistent constraint for a commodity-adjacent ingredient. Fermentation economics and margin structure are not publicly disclosed. No confirmed Series C label or current valuation; funding-stage set to series-c as best-effort from cumulative $137M raised. Beauty and personal care is a smaller, more defensible market than food, where competition on price is fiercer.
