## One-liner

Biomining company using AI-designed enzymes and engineered microbes to dissolve silicate minerals and extract lithium and other critical metals from ore and mining waste, without harsh chemical leaching.

## Problem

Lithium and other critical minerals are recovered by energy-intensive, chemically harsh, and environmentally costly processes (brine evaporation ponds, acid leaching, high-temperature roasting), which are slow, waste-heavy, and often leave metals stranded in low-grade ore and tailings.

## Workaround

Conventional hydrometallurgy and pyrometallurgy: acid or solvent leaching, roasting, and multi-year brine evaporation, plus mining-waste and tailings that are left unprocessed because extracting the residual metal is uneconomic.

## Entry wedge

Discovered a bacterium producing enzymes that dissolve rock to expose embedded metals, then used AI to design an extraction enzyme (LithX) whose binding pocket is fitted to the specific crystal structure of a target mineral, "like a hand into a glove." Targets lithium first, plus a second metal (strontium), positioning to recover metals from ore and mining waste at a lower chemical and energy footprint.

## Method

Ore or mining waste is crushed and ground, then combined with engineered microbes and the designed extraction enzymes in whole-cell or cell-free processes that degrade the silicate matrix to expose and release target metals for separation and recovery. An AI model designs the enzyme to fit the crystal structure of the specific mineral, giving selectivity for the target metal.

## Validated by

Roughly $19M seed round (April 2025) led by Olive Tree Capital, with Y Combinator, TechNexus, Soma Capital, Nomadic Venture Partners, and Liquid 2 Ventures; plus an earlier SBIR grant. Y Combinator S22 cohort; 2022 iGEM Startup Showcase finalist. Grew from a Baltimore basement bench to a 10,000 sq ft lab. Plans to build a plant at a mine site to produce lithium and strontium.

## Competes with

Other biomining and bioleaching players (Endolith, Cemvita, Allonnia, and academic bioleaching groups), direct-lithium-extraction (DLE) companies (Lilac Solutions, EnergyX, Standard Lithium) on the lithium-recovery outcome, and incumbent hydrometallurgical processing.

## Complements

Mining operators and tailings owners that supply ore and waste feedstock, downstream battery-metal refiners and cathode makers who buy recovered lithium, and protein-design and enzyme-engineering tooling upstream.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Mining and ore extraction, mining-waste and tailings generation, enzyme and strain design.
**Upstream of:** Metal refining, battery-material supply chains, and cathode manufacturing.
