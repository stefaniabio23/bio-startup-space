## One-liner

Minimally invasive wearable microsensor for continuous molecular monitoring, starting with glucose and built as a multi-molecule platform.

## Problem

Continuous glucose monitoring proved the value of always-on molecular data, but existing sensors read only glucose and drift over short wear windows. There is no wearable that continuously tracks the broader panel of molecules under the skin.

## Workaround

Finger-prick blood glucose tests or single-analyte CGM patches, plus episodic blood panels for everything else. Users get one molecule at a time and short sensor lifespans.

## Entry wedge

Continuous glucose monitoring, the proven CGM beachhead, positioned around world-first 10-day continuous-wear clinical evidence to differentiate on wear duration and accuracy.

## Method

A proprietary minimally invasive microsensor sits just beneath the skin and detects multiple molecules in real time. The platform is designed so glucose is the first analyte and additional biomarkers follow, "glucose is only the beginning." An independent 46-participant clinical study across Oxford and Cambridge reported 10-day continuous readings.

## TAM logic

CGM alone is a multi-billion-dollar and fast-growing market; a multi-molecule platform extends into broader metabolic and chronic-disease monitoring. A $20B estimate reflects the CGM market plus adjacent continuous molecular monitoring the platform aims to unlock.

## Validated by

$19M Series A (July 2025) co-led by Balderton Capital and Pentland Ventures, with Norrsken VC and JamJar; roughly $32M raised total. Independent 46-participant clinical study (Type 1 and insulin-dependent Type 2 diabetes) at Oxford and Cambridge showing world-first 10-day continuous evidence.

## Competes with

CGM incumbents (Dexcom, Abbott) on the glucose beachhead, and other multi-analyte biosensor platforms (Nutromics, Xsensio, Level Zero Health) on the broader molecular ambition.

## Complements

Diabetes clinics and endocrinologists as clinical channels, and consumer metabolic-health platforms that could surface continuous molecular data once beyond glucose.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** microsensor fabrication and biosensor chemistry.
**Upstream of:** patients and clinicians managing diabetes and, later, other molecular readouts.

## Gaps

Investigational, no MHRA or FDA clearance yet; a pivotal study is planned for 2026 with commercial availability targeted 2027. The multi-molecule claim is unproven beyond glucose. Regulatory route (MHRA vs FDA first) is not specified.
