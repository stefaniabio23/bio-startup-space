## One-liner

Continuous hormone monitoring wearable, a "CGM for hormones" using DNA aptamer sensors in a microneedle patch.

## Problem

Hormones drive fertility, menopause, metabolic and endocrine health, yet they are measured through single blood draws that capture one moment on a curve that swings hour to hour. Enzyme-based CGM chemistry does not generalize to hormones, so there is no continuous readout.

## Workaround

Episodic venous blood draws sent to a lab, at-home urine or saliva strips, or basal body temperature and cycle-tracking proxies. All are intermittent and miss the intraday dynamics that matter for IVF timing and endocrine care.

## Entry wedge

A single-use intermittent device aimed at prescription markets first (IVF and testosterone replacement therapy), before the continuous wearable. The company reported roughly $3M in letters of intent from IVF clinics as early demand proof.

## Method

Aptamers, synthetic single-stranded DNA sequences that fold to bind a specific hormone, are patterned onto a microneedle patch that samples interstitial fluid. Binding produces an electrochemical signal proportional to concentration. Aptamers were chosen over enzymes because they can be raised against many hormone targets. Validated in simulated samples across roughly 98% of the human clinical range for progesterone, estrogen, cortisol and testosterone.

## TAM logic

Hormone health spans fertility and IVF, menopause, endocrine disease, sports and metabolic monitoring. A continuous device sold first into prescription IVF and TRT and later into consumer subscription can attach to markets each worth low single-digit billions. An $8B estimate reflects the addressable slice of global hormone testing plus a continuous-wearable premium.

## Validated by

Roughly €6.6m / $6.9m pre-seed raised February 2025, billed as the largest pre-seed by a European female-founded company, led by redalpine with HAX and Entrepreneur First. Simulated-sample validation across four hormones. About $3M in clinic letters of intent. No regulatory clearance yet.

## Competes with

Other continuous hormone-monitoring entrants profiled alongside it (early-stage), and incumbent episodic hormone testing labs and at-home hormone kits.

## Complements

IVF clinics and endocrinology practices as deployment channels, CGM incumbents whose microneedle and wearable supply chains it can borrow, and femtech tracking apps that could surface the data.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** aptamer selection and microneedle patch manufacturing.
**Upstream of:** clinicians and consumers who act on the hormone curve (IVF trigger timing, HRT and TRT dosing).

## Gaps

No regulatory clearance and no continuous wearable shipping yet; the continuous product is slated for around 2028. Long-term multi-day sensor stability in vivo is unproven outside simulated samples.

## Notes

Founded 2023, London UK. Founders Ula Rustamova (ex-Palantir) and Irene Jia (ex-Philips). Two-phase plan: single-use prescription device first, continuous wearable later.
