## One-liner

Lab-on-Skin wearable platform for continuous, real-time biochemical sensing of proteins, hormones, electrolytes and inflammation markers.

## Problem

Wearables today track physiology (heart rate, motion, temperature) but not biochemistry. Molecular biomarkers that reveal disease state still require blood draws and lab turnaround, so there is no continuous chemical readout at the point of care.

## Workaround

Blood tests processed in central labs, episodic and slow. Physiological wearables act as proxies but cannot measure the actual molecules clinicians care about.

## Entry wedge

A miniaturized CMOS sensing chip built at EPFL, positioned first for clinical decision-making in the hospital setting where continuous biochemical readings can guide care, before consumer wearables.

## Method

Multiple biochemical sensors are integrated onto an ultra-miniaturized CMOS chip that reads target molecules in sweat and skin surface fluid in real time. The chip-level integration is the differentiator, packing lab assays into a wearable footprint. A long-term collaboration with Texas Instruments addresses CMOS integration and manufacturability.

## TAM logic

Continuous biochemical monitoring spans hospital clinical decision support, pharma trial biomarker capture and eventually consumer health. Bridging physiological wearables into molecular sensing opens a slice of the broader remote-monitoring and diagnostics markets; $12B reflects the addressable clinical and consumer biochemical-sensing opportunity.

## Validated by

$7m / €6m oversubscribed Series A closed March 2026, led by WI Harper with Privilège Ventures and the European Innovation Council. Lifetime funding roughly $11.6M. Texas Instruments manufacturing collaboration. EPFL spinout pedigree.

## Competes with

Other biochemical-sensing wearables including sweat-sensing patches (Epicore Biosystems) and microneedle interstitial-fluid platforms (Nutromics, Level Zero Health), though Xsensio targets a broader multi-analyte skin surface readout.

## Complements

Hospital clinical workflows and monitoring dashboards, pharma trial sponsors needing continuous biomarker capture, and semiconductor partners (Texas Instruments) for scale manufacturing.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** CMOS chip fabrication and biosensor surface chemistry.
**Upstream of:** clinicians and health platforms consuming continuous biochemical readouts.

## Gaps

No confirmed FDA or CE clearance; still in clinical validation and pre-commercial. Sweat and skin surface biomarker levels do not always track blood concentrations, a validation hurdle for clinical claims.
