## One-liner

A temple-worn wearable using near-infrared spectroscopy to monitor cerebral blood flow and brain oxygenation in real time.

## Problem

Brain blood flow and oxygenation are central to cognitive and neurological health, yet there is no consumer wearable that tracks them continuously. Existing measurement means bulky clinical NIRS or imaging in a lab.

## Workaround

Clinical near-infrared spectroscopy rigs, functional imaging, or indirect proxies from heart rate and HRV wearables that do not measure the brain directly.

## Entry wedge

A sub-20g temple-worn sensor sold to an early cohort of athletes, founders, scientists, physicians and creators, riding the founder's consumer brand for demand before broader launch.

## Method

Miniaturized medical-grade near-infrared spectroscopy in a temple form factor shines NIR light into cerebral tissue and reads the reflected signal to estimate blood flow and oxygenation. The company markets a derived "real-time autonomic entropy" biomarker.

## TAM logic

Consumer neuro-wellness and brain-health wearables are an emerging category adjacent to the large fitness-wearable market. A $5B estimate reflects a plausible addressable slice of premium brain-monitoring consumer hardware, given the category is unproven.

## Validated by

Roughly $54M raised at ~$190M post-money (announced February 2026), described as a friends-and-family / seed round with Deepinder Goyal contributing ~$25M himself, plus Steadview Capital, Peak XV, Info Edge Ventures and prominent Indian tech angels. First ~100 production units reported ready to ship May 2026.

## Competes with

Consumer neuro and recovery wearables (Muse EEG headbands, WHOOP, Oura on the physiological-proxy side) and clinical NIRS device makers.

## Complements

Sports performance and recovery platforms, longevity and wellness programs, and clinicians who might use continuous cerebral data as research signal.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** NIRS optics and wearable hardware manufacturing.
**Upstream of:** consumers and coaches acting on brain blood-flow trends.

## Gaps

No FDA or CDSCO clearance; framed as experimental / wellness, and its "reverse ageing" and brain-health claims have drawn scientific skepticism. Round is friends-and-family, so stage labeling is soft. Corporate structure sits inside Eternal's Continue Research; standalone HQ unverified.
