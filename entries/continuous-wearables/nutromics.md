## One-liner

"Lab-on-a-Patch," a microneedle DNA-aptamer biosensor for continuous, real-time, multi-analyte molecular monitoring.

## Problem

Critically ill patients need molecules like antibiotics kept in a narrow therapeutic window, but therapeutic drug monitoring relies on intermittent blood draws with slow lab turnaround. By the time a level comes back, the dose may already be wrong, driving toxicity or treatment failure.

## Workaround

Periodic venous blood draws sent to the hospital lab, with hours of delay and no continuous trend. Clinicians dose reactively against stale data.

## Entry wedge

Continuous therapeutic drug monitoring of the antibiotic vancomycin, a high-value hospital use case where getting the dose right quickly matters for kidney safety and survival. First clinical work centered on this single analyte.

## Method

Electrochemical aptamer-based (E-AB) DNA biosensors sit on 3mm microneedles that sample interstitial fluid. Aptamers change conformation on binding their target, producing a measurable current, and multiple aptamer channels multiplex several analytes on one patch. Demonstrated real-time in-vivo dual measurement of vancomycin and tobramycin simultaneously.

## TAM logic

Therapeutic drug monitoring, ICU biomarker surveillance (lactate, creatinine, troponin, sepsis) and continuous molecular monitoring together address a large hospital and critical-care market plus pharma trial use. A $15B estimate reflects the addressable slice of TDM plus continuous critical-care monitoring.

## Validated by

Roughly A$60m raised total (about $40M USD equivalent), including a US$14M Series A in 2022 led by Dexcom Ventures. Pilot-phase vancomycin trial in 85 participants including 27 ICU patients, with data published in Nature Biotechnology (2026). World-first real-time in-vivo dual-antibiotic multiplexing demonstrated in animal models (2025).

## Competes with

Other microneedle interstitial-fluid biosensor platforms (Sava, Level Zero Health) and continuous biochemical wearables (Xsensio), though Nutromics is furthest into hospital therapeutic drug monitoring.

## Complements

Hospital ICUs and pharmacy teams managing antibiotic dosing, CGM incumbents (Dexcom as strategic investor) sharing microneedle and wearable supply chains, and pharma sponsors needing continuous PK data in trials.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** aptamer development and microneedle patch manufacturing.
**Upstream of:** clinicians dosing drugs and monitoring critical-care biomarkers in real time.

## Gaps

Not yet FDA-approved; commercialization still ahead pending US trials. The frequently cited "$60m" total is in AUD (~$40M USD) and round labels are inconsistent across sources. A specific 2028 FDA target was not confirmed in primary sources.

## Notes

Founded 2017, Melbourne Australia with US presence. Founders Peter Vranes and Hitesh Mehta. Dexcom Ventures is the marquee investor.
