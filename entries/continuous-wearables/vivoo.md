## One-liner

FlowPad, a menstrual pad with built-in microfluidic hormone testing that turns a normal cycle day into a passive at-home hormone test.

## Problem

Hormone testing for fertility, PCOS and perimenopause requires a blood draw or a fiddly finger-prick or urine kit on a specific cycle day. Most people skip it, and menstrual blood, which is discarded, actually carries testable hormone signal.

## Workaround

Clinic blood draws timed to the cycle, at-home urine or finger-prick hormone kits, and cycle-tracking apps that infer rather than measure hormones.

## Entry wedge

The FlowPad, a menstrual pad that natively collects menstrual blood and runs an FSH assay in-pad, launched around CES 2026 first to existing Vivoo members, partnered fertility clinics and research institutions. It builds on Vivoo's existing at-home urine test-strip business.

## Method

A two-layer microfluidic pad: a capillary capture layer draws and filters menstrual blood, and a biomarker reaction layer with stabilized reagents produces a visible color change (pregnancy-test-style window). Initial biomarker is FSH, worn on cycle day 2-3 when FSH peaks, read directly or via the Vivoo app.

## TAM logic

Fertility, PCOS and menopause hormone testing is a large and growing femtech segment, and native passive collection could expand testing frequency well beyond current kit users. A $4B estimate reflects the addressable at-home women's hormone testing market.

## Validated by

$6M Series A (Nov 2021) led by Draper Associates, with 500 Startups, Revo Capital, Global Ventures and Halogen Ventures. Existing urine-strip product line with an installed member base. FlowPad debuted at CES 2026 with reported CES Innovation Award recognition.

## Competes with

Qvin (Q-Pad, FDA-cleared menstrual-blood collection pad for HbA1c) on the menstrual-fluid collection form factor, and at-home hormone kit makers (Mira, Modern Fertility, Proov) on fertility hormone testing.

## Complements

Fertility clinics and research institutions as early channels, cycle-tracking apps that could ingest results, and Vivoo's own urine-strip and app ecosystem.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** pad and microfluidic manufacturing plus assay reagent chemistry.
**Upstream of:** consumers and fertility clinicians acting on the hormone result.

## Gaps

FlowPad is positioned as a general wellness product, not FDA-cleared for diagnostic use, and is early-access / pre-market with global launch targeted Q4 2026. Only FSH at launch. Total funding is thin and sources conflict (roughly $6M to $25M); the $6M Series A is the only well-documented round.
