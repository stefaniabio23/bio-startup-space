## One-liner

Indian medtech company making portable, affordable retinal imaging devices with integrated AI diagnostics to screen for preventable blindness at scale, particularly in underserved and rural markets.

## Problem

Most blindness from diabetic retinopathy, glaucoma, and other retinal conditions is preventable if caught early — but access to ophthalmologists and diagnostic equipment is severely limited in India and lower-income countries, where the disease burden is highest.

## Workaround

Traditional fundus cameras cost $15,000-40,000 and require dilated eyes (mydriasis), a dark room, and trained operators. Clinics rely on periodic specialist visits or patient referral to urban centres, leading to late-stage diagnoses.

## Entry wedge

3nethra Classic: a portable, non-mydriatic fundus camera priced for primary care settings in India, enabling screening by non-ophthalmologist operators (optometrists, nurses, health workers). The device was paired with a cloud platform for remote grading by ophthalmologists, creating a tele-ophthalmology network effect.

## Method

Forus Health's 3nethra device line (Classic, Classic HD, Ultima, Specto, Neo, Pico) captures high-resolution fundus and anterior segment images without pupil dilation. Onboard and cloud-based AI engines auto-grade images for diabetic retinopathy, glaucoma, age-related macular degeneration, hypertensive retinopathy, cataracts, ROP, and dry eye. Results are uploaded to a cloud platform for remote ophthalmologist review and patient records. The company runs one of India's largest tele-ophthalmology programs in partnership with Apollo Telehealth.

## TAM logic

The global diabetic retinopathy screening market alone is estimated at $1.5-2B; the broader ophthalmic diagnostics market (glaucoma, AMD, ROP) is $4-5B. Forus targets the underserved segment of this market — primary care settings in emerging markets — which is large in volume but lower in per-test revenue. Global expansion into higher-income markets could substantially increase revenue per procedure. (Source: company materials, forushealth.com)

## Validated by

Series B funding round (November 2025). Inviga Healthcare Fund acquired 21% stake (2025-2026) to scale AI-integrated platforms in India and globally. Total funding: $18.6M from Accel, Chiratae Ventures, Asian Healthcare Fund, TEAMFund, and MedTech Innovator. 3,800+ device installations across 73 countries. 16 million lives impacted. Named among TIME Magazine's World's Top 400 HealthTech Companies (2025). Annual revenue: approximately INR 52.8Cr (~$6.3M USD, FY2025). Published peer-reviewed validation of 3nethra diagnostic accuracy in PubMed.

## Competes with

Topcon (standard fundus cameras, Japan), Optomed (portable fundus cameras, Finland), Remidio (portable retinal imaging, India), EyeNuk (AI diabetic retinopathy screening, US), Retinalyze, IDx-DR (now Digital Diagnostics, FDA-cleared AI grading). Broader competition from Canon and Zeiss medical imaging.

## Complements

Apollo Telehealth (distribution partner), national diabetic retinopathy screening programs, primary care networks in India and Africa, EHR and health records systems in partner hospitals.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Primary care visit or community screening camp, patient registration
**Upstream of:** Specialist ophthalmology consultation, treatment decision (laser, injection, surgery), vision rehabilitation

## Gaps

Revenue per procedure is significantly lower than US/EU diagnostic markets — the affordability model that drives adoption in India limits gross margin. AI regulatory clearance for the US market has not been reported, limiting near-term revenue in the highest-WTP segment. Dependency on government and NGO-driven screening programs creates lumpy demand. Competition from Digital Diagnostics (IDx-DR, FDA cleared since 2018) is entrenched in US primary care.
