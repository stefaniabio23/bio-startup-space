## One-liner

Precision-oncology company using liquid and tissue biopsy tests to support cancer screening, treatment selection, recurrence monitoring, and response tracking.

## Problem

Cancer is most treatable when caught early, but current screening methods (colonoscopy, mammography) are invasive, infrequent, and miss many early-stage cancers.

## Workaround

Colonoscopy and FIT tests for CRC screening, tissue biopsy for treatment selection, CEA and CA-19-9 blood markers for recurrence, all invasive, slow, or insensitive.

## Entry wedge

Guardant360: first FDA-cleared comprehensive liquid biopsy panel for solid tumour genomic profiling (treatment selection in late-stage cancer). Built clinical evidence with oncologists before expanding to screening.

## Method

Sequencing of cell-free tumour DNA fragments shed into blood; proprietary bioinformatics to distinguish tumour signal from background; Shield uses methylation and fragmentomics to detect early-stage CRC.

## TAM logic

Total liquid biopsy market estimated at $6.7B by 2029; broader cancer diagnostics market $45B+. Guardant cites 1.9 million CRC cases globally per year addressable by Shield, and 2M+ patients in the US eligible for Guardant360. (Source: guardanthealth.com/investors)

## Validated by

NASDAQ: GH (public). FDA clearance for Guardant360 CDx (companion diagnostic), FDA approval for Shield (colorectal cancer screening blood test, 2024, first blood-based CRC screening test). ECLIPSE trial: Shield 83% sensitivity for CRC, 90% specificity. Guardant Reveal for MRD monitoring in colorectal cancer.

## Competes with

Foundation Medicine (tissue-based NGS, Roche subsidiary), Illumina (TruSight Oncology), Tempus (multimodal oncology), Exact Sciences (Cologuard, stool-based CRC screening), Grail (Galleri multi-cancer detection).

## Complements

EHR platforms for result delivery, oncology practices for test ordering, pharma for companion diagnostic development (paired with targeted therapies).

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Blood draw or tissue biopsy collection, sequencing infrastructure
**Upstream of:** Treatment selection decisions, trial enrollment (companion diagnostic), recurrence monitoring, drug development (pharma RWE)

## Gaps

Screening reimbursement is the current bottleneck, Shield approval does not guarantee payer coverage or physician ordering at scale. Multi-cancer detection (vs. Grail/Galleri) is the next frontier.

## Notes

HQ: Redwood City, CA. CEO Helmy Eltoukhy (co-founder). Shield approval (July 2024) was the first FDA approval of a blood-based colorectal cancer screening test, a significant regulatory milestone. Guardant360 is the most widely used liquid biopsy in US oncology.
