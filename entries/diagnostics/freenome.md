## One-liner

Multiomics early-cancer-detection company using blood-based signals and machine learning to detect cancer at earlier, more treatable stages.

## Problem

Most cancers are diagnosed at late stage when treatment is less effective; existing screening tests cover only a few cancer types and miss early-stage disease.

## Workaround

Colonoscopy, mammography, PSA testing, and Pap smears, all single-cancer, invasive or limited in sensitivity, and requiring patient compliance with episodic screening.

## Entry wedge

Multi-analyte blood test combining cell-free DNA, RNA, proteins, and immune signals, positioned as more sensitive than single-analyte approaches (cfDNA alone). Genentech partnership (2019) provided capital and clinical access to validate across cancer types.

## Method

Multiomics platform integrating cfDNA, cfRNA, proteomics, and immune profiling from a single blood draw; ML models trained on population cohorts to distinguish cancer from non-cancer signals across tissue types.

## TAM logic

Multi-cancer early detection market is estimated at $30B+ in the US alone at scale (annual screening for 40M+ Americans over 50). Freenome frames the market as a replacement for fragmented, modality-specific cancer screening. (Source: freenome.com/science)

## Validated by

$1.1B raised including Series D ($270M, 2022). Partnerships with Genentech (Roche), Sanofi, and Biogen. PREEMPT CRC trial for colorectal cancer early detection. Over 1M+ biospecimens collected across studies. No FDA clearance yet.

## Competes with

Guardant Health (Shield, ctDNA-based CRC screening), Grail/Illumina (Galleri multi-cancer), Exact Sciences (Cologuard), Foundation Medicine, Tempus.

## Complements

Hospital and health system partners for blood draw collection, oncology practices for follow-up, pharma partners for companion diagnostic development alongside therapy.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Blood draw collection, multiomics sequencing infrastructure
**Upstream of:** Cancer diagnosis, treatment initiation, clinical trial eligibility, early intervention

## Gaps

No FDA clearance yet; clinical validation studies still ongoing. Reimbursement pathway for multi-cancer detection is uncertain. Competing with Grail/Galleri (backed by Illumina) which is further along in multi-cancer detection.

## Notes

HQ: South San Francisco. CEO Aaron Elliott (since April 2025, following Mike Nolan's September 2024 departure). The multiomics approach (vs. cfDNA-only) is the scientific differentiation claim. The Genentech and Sanofi partnerships are the key commercial credibility anchors.
