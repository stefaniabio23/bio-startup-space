## One-liner

Incumbent biosimulation and model-informed drug development platform (Simcyp PBPK, QSP, pharmacometrics) that sets the regulatory-grade bar for in-silico evidence.

## Problem

Drug developers must predict how a compound behaves across diverse patients and justify dose, safety, and design decisions to regulators, which is slow, costly, and uncertain with wet experiments alone.

## Workaround

Empirical clinical studies, in-house pharmacometrics teams, spreadsheet PK models, CRO statistical services, and fragmented point tools.

## Entry wedge

Simcyp PBPK simulation became the de facto standard for predicting drug exposure and drug-drug interactions, widely cited in regulatory submissions, which then anchored a broader software and services suite.

## Method

An integrated end-to-end platform spanning PBPK (Simcyp), quantitative systems pharmacology (Certara IQ, AI-enabled, launched October 2025), pharmacometrics (Phoenix WinNonlin), clinical trial simulation, and toxicology prediction (Libra), backed by validated virtual-patient databases and a large regulatory-science services arm.

## TAM logic

Certara addresses the model-informed drug development software and services market, which it sizes in the low tens of billions given adoption across the top 35 biopharma by R&D spend and 1,600+ organizations across 60 countries. The biosimulation software segment alone is growing ~17% CAGR.

## Validated by

NASDAQ-listed (CERT) since its December 2020 IPO that raised roughly $668M. Used by the top biopharmaceutical companies by R&D spend and referenced extensively in FDA and EMA submissions; ~$233M revenue in the twelve months to September 2020, growing since. Simcyp is a repeatedly regulator-referenced PBPK standard.

## Competes with

Simulations Plus (GastroPlus, ADMET Predictor), VeriSIM Life (hybrid mechanistic AI), FireQSP (QSP model building), plus internal pharma modeling groups and open-source pharmacometrics tools.

## Complements

CROs running the clinical studies that feed and validate models, EDC and clinical data platforms, and regulatory affairs teams assembling submissions.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Preclinical and clinical data generation, target and mechanism biology
**Upstream of:** Dose selection, trial design, regulatory submission, label decisions

## Gaps

Incumbent scale brings legacy software surface area and integration complexity; AI-native challengers (Certara IQ is a response) move faster on individual workflows. Services revenue dilutes pure-software margins. QSP model building remains labor-intensive, the gap FireQSP-style tools target.

## Notes

Formed in 2008 from the merger of Tripos and Pharsight, headquartered in Radnor, PA. Grows through acquisition as well as organic product development. Sets the regulatory-acceptance benchmark that smaller biosimulation and QSP entrants are measured against.
