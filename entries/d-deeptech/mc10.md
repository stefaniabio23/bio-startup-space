## One-liner

Ultra-thin, stretchable skin patch (BioStamp nPoint) that streams clinical-grade physiological data for remote and decentralised trials, now operating inside Medidata Solutions after acquisition.

## Problem

Traditional clinical trials measure patients episodically inside clinics, missing continuous real-world physiology and forcing site visits that raise cost and dropout. Rigid wearables sit awkwardly on the body and cannot capture skin-surface biopotentials like ECG and EMG with research quality.

## Workaround

Holter monitors, actigraphy watches, and periodic in-clinic assessments, each capturing a narrow signal in a fixed setting, plus manual patient diaries prone to recall error.

## Entry wedge

Decentralised and hybrid clinical trials for pharma sponsors. The BioStamp offered a research-grade, conformal alternative to bulky monitors, capturing multiple modalities from a single skin-mounted patch in free-living conditions.

## Method

Serpentine-interconnect silicon nanomembrane circuits are embedded in a thin elastomer that stretches and flexes with skin. The patch records biopotentials, triaxial motion, and temperature, then transmits to a cloud analytics layer that derives digital biomarkers and trial endpoints.

## Validated by

Medidata (a Dassault Systemes company) acquired MC10's digital biomarker business in October 2020, folding the BioStamp nPoint into its Patient Cloud and sensor-integration portfolio. MC10 had raised on the order of USD 100M from investors including North Bridge, Braemar, and corporate partners before the deal. The technology traces to John A. Rogers' epidermal electronics research.

## Competes with

Other decentralised-trial sensor providers (Biofourmis, Koneksa, VivaLNK, iRhythm on the ECG side) and generic consumer wearables adapted for research.

## Complements

Medidata's clinical data platform and eCOA tools, plus CRO trial-operations vendors that need continuous physiological endpoints.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** stretchable-electronics fabrication, biocompatible materials, cloud analytics infrastructure
**Upstream of:** digital endpoint analytics, sponsor decision-making, regulatory submissions

## Gaps

Whether novel digital endpoints derived from skin patches gain regulatory qualification fast enough to justify device and analytics cost versus cheaper consumer wearables; adhesive wear time and compliance remain practical ceilings.

## Notes

MC10's consumer efforts (e.g. WiSP, L'Oreal UV patch) were spun down; the durable value was the clinical digital-biomarker stack, now inside Medidata.
