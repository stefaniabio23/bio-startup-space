## One-liner

Terahertz devices and compact detectors for non-destructive testing and drug, food, and biomedical inspection, using biocompatible GaN and Schottky-diode technology.

## Problem

Inspecting drugs, food, and materials non-destructively without ionizing radiation is hard. X-ray and UV carry safety and material limits, and THz detectors have historically been bulky and expensive.

## Workaround

X-ray, UV, and optical inspection, plus lab-scale THz time-domain spectroscopy that is costly and non-portable.

## Entry wedge

Compact, cost-effective THz detectors (100 GHz to 3 THz) for non-destructive material testing, counterfeit-tablet detection, and food inspection, with biomedical and security use positioned as extensions.

## Method

THz detectors based on InGaAs and GaAs Schottky diodes and gallium nitride (GaN) sources, replacing rare indium and toxic arsenide materials to be biocompatible and human-safe. Applied to non-destructive testing and imaging across the 100 GHz to 3 THz range.

## Validated by

Linked to TU Darmstadt's terahertz "nanovision" research. Public commercial traction (funding, revenue, customers) not disclosed.

## Competes with

Other THz component and imaging vendors (TeraSense, Terahertz Technologies) and X-ray / UV inspection incumbents.

## Complements

Pharma quality-control and anti-counterfeiting workflows, food-safety inspection, and any THz imaging system needing detectors.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** GaN and Schottky-diode semiconductor fabrication.
**Upstream of:** THz imaging systems and inspection users in pharma and food.

## Gaps

Core offering is THz detector hardware for inspection, not a biosensing or diagnostic product. Biomedical use is listed as a future application, not demonstrated. Founding date, funding, and team unverified.
