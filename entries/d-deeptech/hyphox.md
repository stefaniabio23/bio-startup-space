## One-liner

CMOS photonic integrated circuit lab-on-a-chip biosensors that combine optics, electronics, and microfluidics for rapid quantitative testing of pathogens and proteins.

## Problem

Point-of-care and environmental testing still relies on lab-bound assays that are slow and not quantitative. Detecting bacteria, viruses, and proteins in water, urine, or blood quickly and cheaply at scale is unsolved.

## Workaround

Central-lab PCR, culture, and immunoassays with slow turnaround, or rapid tests that are qualitative and low sensitivity.

## Entry wedge

High-volume applications such as water analysis, environmental monitoring, food diagnostics, and point-of-care testing, where a low-cost CMOS chip can undercut lab workflows.

## Method

Biosensors built on photonic integrated circuits in scalable CMOS technology, integrating optical, electrical, and microfluidic components on one chip. Light-based transduction reads analytes captured on the chip surface, producing digitized quantitative real-time data.

## Validated by

Spin-off project of IHP (Innovations for High Performance Microelectronics) in Frankfurt (Oder), Germany. Won the Berlin Brandenburg Innovation Award 2023 over more than 100 applicants.

## Competes with

Delta Diagnostics and SurfixDx (photonic integrated biosensor chips), and CMOS biosensor efforts from larger semiconductor players.

## Complements

IHP's CMOS fabrication base, environmental and point-of-care testing channels, and the broader European integrated-photonics ecosystem.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** IHP CMOS photonics fabrication.
**Upstream of:** point-of-care, environmental, and food-safety testing users.
