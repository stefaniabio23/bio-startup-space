## One-liner

At-home AI skincare device (Longos Sense) that uses quantum-dot QLED sensors to analyze skin and combines several treatment modalities in one system.

## Problem

Professional-grade skin analysis and treatment normally require clinic visits and expensive equipment. Consumers want data-backed, personalized skincare at home.

## Workaround

Dermatology-clinic visits, standalone beauty gadgets, and subjective self-assessment, none of which give quantitative sub-surface skin readouts at home.

## Entry wedge

A consumer device, Longos Sense, that bundles quantum-dot sensing with RF, LED therapy, ultrasonic vibration, and cooling/heating cycles, sold with an AI personalization app.

## Method

Quantum-dot (QLED) sensors detect sub-surface changes in moisture, collagen density, and pigmentation within seconds, and quantum-dot light filtering focuses treatment wavelengths onto the skin. An AI layer personalizes the analysis and treatment plan. The company has supplied quantum-dot wide-range sensors and imaging materials since 2017.

## Validated by

Consumer product shipping with an AI skincare app on Google Play. Says it works with dermatologists, biophotonics engineers, and material scientists. No disclosed funding or clinical trials found.

## Competes with

Consumer beauty-tech and at-home skin-analysis devices (HiMirror-style analyzers, LED/RF home devices) and clinic-based skin diagnostics.

## Complements

Consumer skincare brands, dermatology-adjacent services, and quantum-dot sensor/material supply relationships.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** quantum-dot sensor and imaging-material manufacturing.
**Upstream of:** consumers doing at-home skin analysis and treatment.
