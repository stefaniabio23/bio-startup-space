## One-liner

Clinical-stage oncology company developing nanoscale metal-organic framework radioenhancers (RiMO) that amplify radiotherapy in tumours and combine with immunotherapy and chemotherapeutics.

## Problem

External-beam radiotherapy is limited by the dose healthy tissue can tolerate, so many tumours are undertreated, and radiotherapy alone often fails to trigger a systemic anti-tumour immune response.

## Workaround

Dose escalation and fractionation, external radiosensitisers, and separate immunotherapy or chemotherapy regimens that are not spatially concentrated in the tumour.

## Entry wedge

Intratumorally injected hafnium-based nMOF (RiMO-301) that locally amplifies radiation dose, starting in advanced solid tumours and head and neck cancer, including combinations with PD-1 inhibitors.

## Method

Nanoscale metal-organic frameworks built from high-Z metal clusters (hafnium) absorb X-rays and generate reactive oxygen species, concentrating radiation damage inside the tumour. The porous MOF scaffold can also load chemotherapeutics and pair with checkpoint inhibitors, turning the tumour into an in situ immune-activating site. Technology licensed from Wenbin Lin's lab at the University of Chicago.

## Validated by

Phase 1 dose-escalation trial of RiMO-301 with palliative radiotherapy (with or without pembrolizumab) reported as well tolerated with promising efficacy signals; Phase 1b/2a in recurrent or metastatic head and neck cancer; ASCO-published results; CPI-200 program dosing patients.

## Competes with

Nanobiotix (NBTXR3 hafnium-oxide radioenhancer, the closest analog), Curadel and other radiosensitiser developers, and immuno-oncology combination programs.

## Complements

Radiation oncology centres, checkpoint-inhibitor makers for combination regimens, and pharma partners for late-stage development and commercialisation.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** MOF chemistry and University of Chicago nMOF IP, radiosensitiser and drug supply
**Upstream of:** radiation oncology treatment, immuno-oncology combination therapy, pharma licensing

## Gaps

Randomised efficacy data beyond early phase, differentiation versus Nanobiotix NBTXR3, disclosed funding, and manufacturing scale-up for a nanoscale MOF injectable.

## Notes
