## One-liner

Clinical-stage biowearable that continuously monitors proteins and electrolytes such as NT-proBNP, potassium, sodium, and creatinine from interstitial fluid to manage heart failure in real time.

## Problem

Heart failure is managed on sparse, episodic lab draws and clinic visits, so decompensation is caught late and readmissions are high. There is no continuous readout of the molecular signals, natriuretic peptides, electrolytes, renal markers, that actually drive management.

## Workaround

Periodic venous blood panels, weight and symptom tracking, implantable pulmonary-artery pressure sensors (CardioMEMS), and continuous glucose monitors that read only glucose.

## Entry wedge

A wearable heart-failure monitor (HF Monitor) worn for 10 to 14 days that streams continuous biomarker data to clinicians, targeting the high-cost, high-urgency problem of heart-failure readmissions.

## Method

A thin, flexible probe sits in the dermis and extracts interstitial fluid analytes, which are read by analyte-specific aptamer molecular switches paired with a miniaturised optoelectronic reader. Data streams to the cloud for provider access. Commercialises continuous molecular biosensing from H. Tom Soh's Stanford lab plus microfabrication from Joseph DeSimone's lab. Demonstrated glucose, potassium, lactate, cortisol, and NT-proBNP at physiologic concentrations.

## Validated by

Peer-reviewed work in JACC: Basic to Translational Science on continuous molecular monitoring for heart failure; first-in-human continuous multi-day free-cortisol data presented at ADA 2026; $14M seed (led by Interlagos, with Stanford and Overwater Ventures) bringing total funding to about $23M.

## Competes with

Abbott (CardioMEMS implantable HF sensor), continuous glucose monitor makers (Dexcom, Abbott) extending into multi-analyte, Nutromics (ISF molecular monitoring), and Profusa on the biosensor side.

## Complements

Heart-failure clinics and cardiology practices, remote patient monitoring platforms, payers focused on readmission reduction, and AI models that consume continuous biomarker streams.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** aptamer selection, microfabrication, optoelectronic reader hardware
**Upstream of:** heart-failure clinical decision support, remote patient monitoring, value-based cardiology care

## Gaps

FDA clearance, multi-day sensor accuracy and stability for protein analytes in real patients, reimbursement pathway, and proof that continuous NT-proBNP changes outcomes versus standard care.

## Notes

Menlo Park, California. Stanford spinout (Soh and DeSimone labs). The multi-analyte, protein-capable angle is the differentiator versus glucose-only CGM. Uses aptamer electrochemical/optical switches plus a dermal ISF probe, mapping to both the electrochemical-aptamer-biosensing and microneedle-isf-sensing substrates.
