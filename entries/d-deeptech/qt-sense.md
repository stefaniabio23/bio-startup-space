## One-liner

Fluorescent nanodiamond quantum sensors introduced into living cells to measure free radicals and oxidative stress in real time with single-cell precision.

## Problem

Oxidative stress and free-radical activity drive disease and drug response, but they are transient and hard to measure inside living cells. Existing probes are indirect, destructive, or lack single-cell, real-time resolution.

## Workaround

Fluorescent dyes and ensemble assays for reactive oxygen species, which bleach, perturb the cell, average over populations, and miss real-time kinetics.

## Entry wedge

Selling the nanodiamond quantum-sensing platform to pharma and academic labs for mechanism-of-action and oxidative-stress studies, including mapping metabolic vulnerabilities in tumor models under the ONCO-Q grant.

## Method

Fluorescent nanodiamonds carrying nitrogen-vacancy (NV) centers are introduced into living cells. The NV centers' optical properties shift with local magnetic noise from unpaired electrons in free radicals, and confocal microscopy plus quantum relaxometry reads this in situ without destroying the cell. The same nanodiamonds also enable magnetometry and pH-sensitive theranostic work.

## Validated by

Raised EUR 4M (about USD 4.7M) in 2026. Used to demonstrate mechanism of action for FDA-approved compounds and to map oxidative stress in colorectal tumor models under the ONCO-Q grant. Underpinned by peer-reviewed nanodiamond quantum-sensing work (in vivo free-radical sensing in C. elegans, Advanced Science 2025).

## Competes with

Fluorescent ROS probe makers and other intracellular sensing approaches; few direct NV-in-cell commercial peers.

## Complements

Pharma drug-mechanism and toxicology programs, oncology metabolism research, and academic redox-biology labs.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** nanodiamond fabrication, NV quantum-optics instrumentation, and surface chemistry.
**Upstream of:** pharma and academic researchers studying oxidative stress and drug mechanism.

## Gaps

Early commercial stage; a specialized measurement rather than a standardized high-throughput assay. Funding is a single EUR 4M round. Founding year approximate.

## Notes

Netherlands (Groningen lineage, University of Groningen / UMCG). Uses genuine NV-center quantum sensing in fluorescent nanodiamonds, a clean match to the nv-diamond-quantum-sensing primitive.
