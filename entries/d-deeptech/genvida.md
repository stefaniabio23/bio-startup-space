## One-liner

Hong Kong company building the SONAS solid-state nanopore sensor array into an automated lab-on-a-chip for genome sequencing and single-molecule sensing.

## Problem

Biological nanopores are sensitive and short-lived; sequencing still requires separate sample-prep steps and instruments. A robust, integrated, on-site sequencing chip does not exist at scale.

## Workaround

Oxford Nanopore's biological-pore devices, Illumina short-read platforms, and Chinese nanopore entrants (Qitan, AxBio) using biological or CMOS approaches.

## Entry wedge

A globally patented solid-state nanopore array (SONAS) that runs smart sample prep, sequencing, and single-molecule identification in a fully automated lab-on-a-chip with a cloud bioinformatics suite, aimed at real-time on-site diagnostics.

## Method

Solid-state (non-biological) nanopore sensor arrays detect molecules translocating through fabricated pores; the SONAS platform integrates sample preparation, sequencing, and single-molecule sensing into one automated chip, with cloud-based analysis. Solid-state pores promise greater durability than protein pores.
