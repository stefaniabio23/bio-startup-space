## One-liner

Develops molecularly imprinted polymers, synthetic antibodies, as stable low-cost recognition reagents for in vitro diagnostics and cell and gene therapy workflows.

## Problem

Antibodies are the default recognition reagent in diagnostics but they are expensive, batch-variable, animal-derived, and need cold-chain storage. That makes them a fragile and costly foundation for high-volume or point-of-care tests.

## Workaround

Recombinant antibodies, aptamers, and affinity proteins reduce some antibody drawbacks but each carries its own stability, cost, or selection limitations, and antibodies remain the incumbent.

## Entry wedge

Supplying molecularly imprinted polymer binders as drop-in synthetic antibody replacements for specific diagnostic and bioprocessing targets where antibody cost, stability, or supply chain is a pain point.

## Method

Peptides representing identified surface epitopes are immobilised on a solid-phase support. Functional monomers form pre-polymerisation complexes around the immobilised peptide and are locked in during controlled polymerisation, then released by elution. The result is a polymer with a binding pocket templated to the target, with control over affinity and batch-to-batch consistency. Francesco Canfarotta, a molecularly imprinted polymer specialist, leads the chemistry.
