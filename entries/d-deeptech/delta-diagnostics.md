## One-liner

Photonic integrated circuit biosensors (product inQuiQ) that measure a panel of biomarkers label-free and in real time on a low-cost chip and reader.

## Problem

Measuring concentrations of many biomolecules at once is slow, label-heavy, and expensive. Life-science researchers and eventual point-of-care users want fast, quantitative, multiplexed readouts without fluorescent tagging or large lab instruments.

## Workaround

Fluorescence assays (ELISA), mass spectrometry, and plate readers. These need labels, dedicated labs, and hours of turnaround, and they scale poorly to cheap decentralized measurement.

## Entry wedge

Life-science research and drug-discovery labs that need affordable, label-free, multiplexed biomarker measurement. Selling the inQuiQ instrument plus disposable sensor chips into research before pushing toward regulated diagnostics.

## Method

Nanophotonic Evanescent-field Sensing (NES) on photonic integrated circuits. Light travels through waveguides on the chip, and biomolecules captured on the functionalized surface shift the light's properties, giving a label-free real-time signal. Multiple sensor channels on one chip detect up to 16 biomolecules simultaneously, paired with a compact readout instrument.

## Validated by

Spun out of TNO's optics department in 2018 after roughly a decade of research. Seed investment from TNO and PhotonDelta in 2022, then a EUR 5.25M Series A in 2023. Part of the Dutch PhotonDelta integrated-photonics ecosystem.

## Competes with

SurfixDx and HyPhoX (photonic integrated biosensor chips), plus label-free platforms such as SPR (Biacore) and Bio-Layer Interferometry.

## Complements

Drug-discovery and biomarker-research workflows, the PhotonDelta / LioniX photonics chip supply chain, and eventual point-of-care diagnostic developers licensing the sensing layer.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** integrated-photonics chip fabrication and surface functionalization chemistry.
**Upstream of:** researchers and diagnostic developers who need quantitative multiplexed biomarker readouts.

## Gaps

Not a regulated diagnostic yet; current wedge is research tools. Total funding is approximate (seed plus EUR 5.25M Series A). Now operating under the Delta Life Science brand, which can cause naming confusion.

## Notes

Rotterdam, Netherlands. TNO spin-off. CEO Guy Dewil. Rebranded from Delta Diagnostics to Delta Life Science; product line marketed as inQuiQ label-free multiplexed biosensing.
