## One-liner

Chinese nanopore sequencing company shipping the QNome, QPursue, and QPinnacle device families across low, medium, and high throughput.

## Problem

Nanopore sequencing was effectively a single-vendor (Oxford Nanopore) global monopoly, leaving Chinese research, clinical, and public-health users dependent on imported instruments and consumables.

## Workaround

Imported Oxford Nanopore devices, Illumina short-read platforms, and other Chinese sequencing entrants (BGI/MGI short-read, GeneMind).

## Entry wedge

A domestically developed handheld sequencer (QNome-3841, launched December 2021) making Qitan the first Chinese firm and second in the world to commercialise fourth-generation nanopore sequencing, then expanding upward into medium and high throughput.

## Method

Single-molecule nanopore sequencing: strands ratchet through a protein pore over a sensor array, producing ionic-current signals decoded into long-read DNA and RNA sequence. QNome is handheld and low-throughput; QPursue is medium-throughput; QPinnacle2 (Sept 2025) is high-throughput.

## Validated by

Raised RMB 700M (~USD 104M) Series C in December 2022 led by Meituan, following RMB 400M+ Series B (2021) and RMB 100M+ Series A (2020). By September 2025, 300+ QNome and 200+ QPursue users deployed across research, clinical, public-health, forensic, agricultural, and environmental settings in China. Published forensic microhaplotype sequencing on QNome.

## Competes with

Oxford Nanopore (direct nanopore competitor and category owner), AxBio and Genvida (other Chinese nanopore entrants), Illumina and MGI/BGI (short-read), PacBio (long-read HiFi).

## Complements

Domestic Chinese bioinformatics and clinical-interpretation providers, sample-prep suppliers, and public-health surveillance programs.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** protein-pore and enzyme engineering, chip fabrication, nucleic-acid extraction
**Upstream of:** genome assembly, clinical diagnostics, forensic and public-health analysis

## Gaps

Raw accuracy and the consumable/software ecosystem still trail Oxford Nanopore; international expansion beyond China is limited and faces IP and geopolitical friction.

## Notes

The leading domestic challenger to Oxford Nanopore inside China, framed by observers as a "two-horse race" for nanopore's future. Completed a low-mid-high throughput product matrix in 2025, a milestone few nanopore firms globally have reached. Total funding is an estimate summing disclosed A/B/C rounds.
