## One-liner

Dubai deep-tech building smart contact lenses from atom-thin 2D and MXene materials that merge non-invasive health monitoring, biosensing, and extended-reality vision in a single lens.

## Problem

Health monitoring and augmented displays live in bulky external devices. There is no continuous, invisible, always-on interface that reads the body and augments vision from the eye itself.

## Workaround

Smart glasses and headsets for display, fingerstick and CGM devices for glucose, and tonometry visits for intraocular pressure. All are separate, intermittent, or intrusive.

## Entry wedge

High-value continuous health readings, glucose from tear fluid and intraocular pressure for glaucoma, as early single-function medical lenses, alongside the longer-horizon extended-reality lens. Multiple working prototypes were shown at MWC 2026.

## Method

Uses atom-thin 2D materials including MXenes to build sensors, optics, and electronics thin and flexible enough for a contact lens. A miniature electrochemical sensor reads tear glucose and transmits wirelessly; an embedded optical pattern reacts to intraocular pressure. The same materials platform underpins the extended-reality display roadmap. Founded by Roman Axelrod and physicist Valentyn Volkov.

## Validated by

Five working smart contact lens prototypes demonstrated at MWC 2026; a $250M Series A at a reported $1.35B valuation, the largest Series A in MENA and among the largest hardware rounds globally, providing capital to move from research to hardware.

## Competes with

Mojo Vision (smart contact lens, pivoted), Google/Verily earlier glucose-lens efforts, Sensimed Triggerfish (IOP-sensing lens), and smart-glasses makers on the display side.

## Complements

Smartphones as the data hub, glaucoma and diabetes care pathways for the medical lenses, and the broader XR ecosystem for the display product.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** 2D and MXene materials synthesis, microfabrication, ultra-low-power electronics
**Upstream of:** continuous consumer health data, diabetes and glaucoma monitoring, XR interface applications

## Gaps

Independent proof of tear-glucose accuracy versus blood, ocular safety and comfort over long wear, power and data solutions in-lens, and a credible regulatory path for the medical-sensing claims. Consumer-ready prototype targeted for 2027.

## Notes

The MXene / 2D-materials angle is the technical core and the join to this substrate; the company also markets an XR display vision. Very capital-heavy and prototype-stage, so commercialisation status is research_only despite the large raise. Founder-brand and capital are as much of the moat as the science at this stage.
