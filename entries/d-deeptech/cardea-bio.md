## One-liner

Builds graphene field-effect transistor biosensors, including the CRISPR-Chip, that read unamplified genomic DNA and biomolecules electronically without PCR, in minutes.

## Problem

Molecular diagnostics depend on PCR amplification, which is slow, contamination-prone, hard to quantify, and needs central-lab infrastructure. Reading intact genomic DNA directly and electronically has been out of reach.

## Workaround

PCR and qPCR, next-generation sequencing, optical CRISPR assays (SHERLOCK, DETECTR) that still often need amplification, and lateral-flow tests with limited quantitation.

## Entry wedge

Selling the Cardean transistor and CRISPR-Chip platform to researchers and biotech and pharma partners for amplification-free digital genomics and biosensing co-development, rather than a single finished diagnostic.

## Method

Proprietary graphene field-effect transistors (Cardean Transistors) use biology as the transistor gate. In the CRISPR-Chip, a deactivated CRISPR-Cas complex immobilised on the graphene gate searches genomic DNA and shifts the transistor current on target binding, giving amplification-free electronic detection. The same platform reads proteins and other analytes electronically. Commercialises CRISPR-FET work associated with Kiana Aran.

## Validated by

CRISPR-Chip paper in Nature Biomedical Engineering (most-read of its year, cover story) demonstrating detection of unamplified target genes; SNP-Chip for single-point-mutation detection; multiple international patents on graphene biosensors; $7.5M Series A2 first close.

## Competes with

Sherlock Biosciences and Mammoth Biosciences (CRISPR diagnostics), Nanomix, Roswell Biotechnologies (molecular electronics), and Oxford Nanopore on amplification-free readout.

## Complements

Diagnostics and biotech developers building assays on the platform, pharma R&D and QC (for example CRISPR guide-to-Cas quality control), and academic genomics labs.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** graphene fabrication, CRISPR-Cas reagents, semiconductor manufacturing
**Upstream of:** amplification-free diagnostic assays, digital genomics research tools, biosensing product developers

## Gaps

Clinical-grade sensitivity versus PCR at scale, manufacturing consistency of graphene FETs, integrated sample-to-answer cartridges, and clarity on funding and commercial traction after a quiet period.

## Notes
