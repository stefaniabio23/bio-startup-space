## One-liner

Reusable wearable sweat sensor that measures fluid and sodium loss in real time and turns it into personalised hydration guidance for endurance athletes.

## Problem

Athletes hydrate blind. Sweat rate and sodium concentration vary widely by person, effort, and conditions, and there is no easy way to measure them during training, so fueling and hydration are guesswork built on generic tables.

## Workaround

Sweat patches sent to a lab for one-off analysis, body-mass measurement before and after exercise, and generic hydration calculators. None give a live signal while training.

## Entry wedge

Elite and serious endurance sport (cycling, running, triathlon). The device earned UCI approval and was adopted by professional teams, using the credibility of top-tier sport as a beachhead before broader consumer fitness.

## Method

An on-skin electrochemical sensor with an integrated microfluidic channel collects and analyses sweat as it is produced, combined with temperature and ambient sensing. The reusable sensor syncs to a companion app that computes personalised fluid and sodium loss and hydration recommendations. FLOWBIO claims independent validation as the most accurate sweat sensor available.

## Validated by

Independent validation study cited by the company claiming lab-grade precision; UCI approval for use in competitive cycling; adoption by professional cycling, running, and triathlon teams.

## Competes with

Nix Biosensors (single-use sweat patch), Gatorade Gx sweat patch, hDrop, Epicore Biosystems (Gatorade Gx partner), and traditional lab sweat testing services.

## Complements

Sports nutrition and electrolyte brands, training platforms (Strava, TrainingPeaks), bike computers and multisport watches that could ingest hydration data, and team performance staff.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** electrochemical sensor and microfluidic component supply, wearable hardware manufacturing
**Upstream of:** athlete hydration decisions, sports nutrition product recommendations, training and performance platforms

## Gaps

Whether continuous sweat electrolyte data changes performance outcomes enough to sustain consumer willingness to pay beyond a niche of elite athletes, and whether the reusable form factor holds accuracy over long use.

## Notes

Founded by Stefan van der Fluit and Giovanni Rovere, London-based, SOSV and Boost VC among 15 investors. First prototype 2021, first reusable sensor 2023, commercial FLOWBIO Sensor launched 2025. Positioned squarely as consumer sports wellness, not a medical device, which keeps regulatory burden light but caps the addressable clinical value.
