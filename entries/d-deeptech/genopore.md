## One-liner

Digital protein identification and quantification using solid-state nanopore translocation plus AI-powered optical readout, aiming to replace mass spec and affinity assays.

## Problem

Proteomics still depends on mass spectrometry (complex, low-throughput on low-abundance proteins) or affinity panels (limited to known targets). There is no simple, direct, whole-protein digital identification method.

## Workaround

Mass-spectrometry proteomics, affinity assays (Olink, SomaScan, antibody arrays), and enzymatic peptide sequencing.

## Entry wedge

Whole-protein identification without mass spec, using chemo-selective labeling and optical detection at the single-molecule level to give a digital, count-based proteomics readout.

## Method

Chemo-selective protein labeling, translocation of labeled proteins through a solid-state nanopore, and AI-powered optical single-molecule readout combine to identify and quantify intact proteins directly, without the complexity of mass spectrometry or the target-limits of affinity methods.
