## One-liner

Startup using dielectric metasurfaces to shrink an imaging system to hair-width optics inside a biopsy needle, giving physicians real-time guidance to detect small lung tumors in the narrowest airways.

## Problem

Lung biopsies of small peripheral nodules are often performed effectively blind, guided only by pre-procedure imaging, so the needle can miss the tumor, forcing repeat procedures and delaying diagnosis. Conventional optics are too large to image from inside the narrowest airways.

## Workaround

CT-guided and electromagnetic-navigation bronchoscopy plus fluoroscopy, which localise a region but do not confirm cellular-level tissue at the needle tip in real time.

## Entry wedge

Early lung-cancer detection: a probe thin enough to thread through the smallest airways and integrate with biopsy needles, providing real-time imaging guidance during the biopsy itself.

## Method

Proprietary dielectric-metasurface optics collapse imaging elements to the scale of a human hair, forming a probe that travels deep into peripheral airways. The optics deliver real-time in-vivo tissue images at the needle tip so clinicians can target suspicious tissue directly instead of sampling blindly.

## Validated by

Founded 2021; raised a USD 5M oversubscribed seed round (announced May 2023) led by MetaVC Partners with SOSV, Sony Innovation Fund, TSVC, ENEA, and angels including Dropbox co-founder Arash Ferdowsi. Received follow-on investment from Applied Ventures (Applied Materials' venture arm) to accelerate the lung-cancer product.

## Competes with

Navigation-bronchoscopy and robotic-bronchoscopy platforms (Intuitive Ion, Medtronic superDimension/Illumisite), confocal and OCT endomicroscopy vendors, and other early lung-cancer detection approaches.

## Complements

Bronchoscopy and biopsy-workflow makers, pathology and liquid-biopsy diagnostics downstream, and metasurface-optics fabrication suppliers.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** dielectric-metasurface fabrication, endoscopic-device engineering, imaging-sensor components
**Upstream of:** interventional pulmonology procedures, tissue diagnosis, treatment decisions

## Gaps

Everything depends on clinical validation and FDA clearance for an in-vivo interventional imaging device, a long, capital-intensive path on a modest seed round; commercial timing and whether image guidance measurably improves biopsy yield remain unproven.

## Notes

Thin-data entry: single disclosed seed round and limited public detail, so TRL and TAM are estimates. Strong example of the metamaterials-metasurfaces primitive applied directly to a clinical diagnostic (D.2/D.3) rather than consumer optics.
