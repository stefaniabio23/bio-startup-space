## One-liner

Japanese smart-apparel maker whose e-skin fabric embeds stretchable sensors into clothing to track full-body motion, sleep, and vitals without cameras.

## Problem

Camera-based motion capture needs a fixed studio and constant line of sight, and rigid wearables measure one point on the body. Continuous whole-body physiological monitoring in daily life, especially for elderly care, has no comfortable, unobtrusive form factor.

## Workaround

Optical motion-capture suits with markers, wrist-worn fitness trackers, bed sensors, and camera monitoring, each limited in coverage, comfort, or privacy.

## Entry wedge

e-skin Sleep & Lounge smart pyjamas for elderly care, tracking sleep, activity, and falls, plus e-skin motion-capture and EMS apparel for fitness and research.

## Method

A proprietary Printed Circuit Fabric (PCF) weaves stretchable conductive traces and sensors directly into washable garments. Distributed strain and biopotential sensors capture posture, movement, and respiration, streaming to a companion app and analytics layer.

## Validated by

Spun out of the University of Tokyo in November 2015 by Ichiro Amimori and colleagues; raised roughly USD 3.5M across seed rounds from Beyond Next Ventures, UTokyo IPC, JST, and corporate CVCs including apparel maker AOKI. Products shipping to consumers and care settings in Japan; recognised in electronic-skin market surveys.

## Competes with

Consumer wearables (Apple Watch, Oura, Whoop), other smart-textile firms (Myant, Sensoria, Nextiles), and camera or bed-based sleep monitors.

## Complements

Elderly-care providers, sleep-analytics platforms, and rehabilitation and fitness services that consume continuous garment-derived data.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** stretchable-fabric materials, textile manufacturing, sensor electronics
**Upstream of:** consumer health apps, elderly-care monitoring services, motion-analytics platforms

## Gaps

Consumer smart apparel has repeatedly struggled to reach durable scale; the open question is whether care and clinical channels provide enough sustained willingness to pay to outlast the low-margin consumer novelty trap. Thin funding relative to hardware ambition.

## Notes

Thin-data entry: funding figures span 2016-2020 rounds and totals vary by source (roughly USD 3-3.5M disclosed). Positioned more as consumer and elder-care apparel than regulated medical device. Useful as a textile-electronics counterpoint to skin-patch epidermal electronics like MC10.
