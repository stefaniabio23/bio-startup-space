## One-liner

Partners with health systems and providers to operationalize value-based care, delivering the technology, data analytics, and clinical infrastructure needed to succeed in shared-risk payment models.

## Problem

Health systems want to participate in value-based care and shared-risk contracts, but lack the operational infrastructure, real-time data pipelines, and clinical change management capability to execute. Most organizations stall at the pilot stage.

## Workaround

Consulting firms (Evolent Health, Aledade, Privia Health), internal population health teams, and standalone analytics tools that do not integrate into clinical workflow.

## Entry wedge

Founded by Adam Boehler and Abe Sutton via Rubicon Founders, both former senior CMMI leaders with direct policy relationships and credibility with health system executives. The founder network provided access to early health system partners without a standard sales cycle.

## Method

Honest embeds technology-enabled care coordination, predictive analytics, and a clinical operations layer inside partner health systems. Providers receive real-time risk stratification, care gap alerts, and proactive outreach tools. The platform extends care into homes and communities through clinical models that reduce avoidable utilization. Honest manages performance in value-based and shared-risk contracts on behalf of partners, including quality measurement and cost tracking.

## TAM logic

The U.S. healthcare system spends roughly $4.5 trillion annually; value-based care arrangements now cover an estimated $1 trillion+ in spend. The addressable market for VBC enablement technology and services sits in the tens of billions. No independent TAM figure was found on the Honest Health website; $50B is an estimate based on the broader VBC services market.

## Validated by

$140M capital raise announced February 2026, led by NewSpring Healthcare with participation from Oak HC/FT, Welsh Carson, Durable Capital, and K2 HealthVentures. Active partnership with Albany Med Health System (April 2025). More than 115,000 Medicare and Medicare Advantage members under management as of 2024. CEO Robert Bessler, MD appointed April 2024.

## Competes with

Aledade, Privia Health, Evolent Health, Vitalink Health, Navvis, Cano Health (bankrupt 2024), Iora Health (acquired by One Medical).

## Complements

EHR vendors (Epic, Oracle Cerner) for data integration, Medicare Advantage plans for shared-savings contracts, remote patient monitoring platforms for home-based care extension.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** EHR vendors, claims data aggregators, payer attribution systems
**Upstream of:** Specialist referral networks, post-acute care providers, patient-facing care management apps

## Gaps

The $140M raise appears to be the first publicly disclosed large institutional round, making total historical funding opaque. Revenue model relies on shared savings, which is susceptible to Medicare Advantage rate pressure and CMS policy changes. Physician adoption of real-time alert tools is historically low without heavy change management investment. Scaling across health systems with heterogeneous EHR environments is operationally expensive. No published clinical outcomes data comparing Honest-managed populations to controls at time of research.

## Notes

Rubicon Founders co-founder Abe Sutton currently leads CMMI, creating a policy adjacency that is both a moat and a potential conflict-of-interest risk. CEO Robert Bessler previously founded Sound Physicians, suggesting operational depth in physician management. Jason Howard joined as President in October 2025 with 25+ years of healthcare leadership experience. Honest appears to operate in the same market segment as Evolent Health but positions specifically for health systems rather than independent physician groups.
