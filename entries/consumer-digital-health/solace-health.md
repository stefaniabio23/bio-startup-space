## One-liner

Connects patients with trained healthcare advocates who navigate insurance, coordinate care, and attend appointments on their behalf, covered by Medicare and commercial insurance.

## Problem

Patients are unable to navigate the complexity of the U.S. healthcare system on their own, even when highly educated. Fragmented care, opaque insurance rules, long specialist waits, and billing errors lead to delayed treatment, worse outcomes, and financial stress.

## Workaround

Patients rely on family members as informal advocates, hospital patient navigators (limited scope), social workers, or simply give up. Online research and call-center-based insurance support are widely used but deliver poor outcomes.

## Entry wedge

Founder Jeremy Gurewitz built the original concept from personal experience advocating for his mother, a radiologist, through a pancreatic cancer diagnosis. The founding story gave early media traction and shaped the product around the most complex, high-stakes navigation scenarios. Medicare reimbursement was the initial commercial wedge, making advocates billable without out-of-pocket cost to patients.

## Method

Patients complete a free care assessment, then Solace matches them with a dedicated advocate based on clinical need and geography. Advocates communicate via phone, text, and secure messaging. Core services include: specialist referral and appointment scheduling, insurance prior authorization and appeals, billing dispute resolution, care visit accompaniment by phone or video, and translation of clinical information into plain language. Employer-sponsored plans now also use Solace as a benefit add-on, with aggregate usage data shared with employers but no individual patient details.

## TAM logic

Solace frames its market as the full U.S. healthcare navigation opportunity. The Medicare patient advocacy reimbursement code alone covers a large population; the commercial employer market adds further scale. The broader patient navigation and care coordination market has been estimated at $20-30B. Figure above is an estimate; no independent TAM figure was found on the company website.

## Validated by

$130M Series C led by IVP in February 2026, valuing company at over $1B. Prior rounds: $14M Series A (Inspired Capital, Craft Ventures, Torch Capital), $60M Series B (Menlo Ventures, April 2025). 2,000+ advocates as of February 2026. 20,000+ patients served per month. Company claims 98% of patients feel more in control post-advocacy and 92% report measurably better health outcomes (methodology not independently verified).

## Competes with

Accolade Health, Quantum Health, Included Health, Transcarent, healthcare navigation features built into large payer (Aetna, United) member portals.

## Complements

Primary care platforms (One Medical, Oak Street), EHR patient portal vendors, Medicare Advantage plans seeking supplemental navigation benefits, employer benefits consultants and brokers.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Diagnosis/referral initiation at primary care or specialist level, payer enrollment and benefits administration
**Upstream of:** Specialist appointments, surgical consultations, ongoing chronic care management

## Gaps

The human advocate model creates a direct labor cost that scales linearly with patient volume, making unit economics dependent on reimbursement rates that could change. Reported outcome data ("92% measurably better health outcomes") lacks published methodology or peer-reviewed validation. The $1B valuation implies significant growth expectations that depend on employer and payer channel adoption beyond Medicare. Solace does not replace clinical care, limiting its ability to improve outcomes in conditions requiring earlier medical intervention. Retention economics post-acute episode are unclear.

## Notes

Co-founded by Jeremy Gurewitz (CEO) and Sara Sargent (CPO). Based in California. IVP led the Series C; Menlo Ventures led Series B. The company is explicitly framing advocacy as a "standard of care" rather than a premium service, which is a category-creation positioning strategy. The expansion into commercial insurance was announced alongside the Series C and is a meaningful revenue diversification from the Medicare-only origin.
