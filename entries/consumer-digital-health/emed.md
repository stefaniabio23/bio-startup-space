## One-liner

Delivers clinician-led GLP-1 weight management programs to employers and their employees, using agentic AI to drive adherence above industry norms.

## Problem

GLP-1 medications (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro) have strong clinical efficacy but suffer from high discontinuation rates, typically 50-60% within 12 months, and high cost without employer subsidy. Most employer-sponsored programs lack clinical wraparound to sustain adherence.

## Workaround

Employer PBM carve-outs managing GLP-1 access without clinical support, direct-to-consumer telehealth prescribers (Ro, Hims), standalone weight management apps, and traditional employer wellness programs with low engagement.

## Entry wedge

eMed launched in 2020 as a digital-first at-home COVID-19 testing platform, partnering with the NIH and Quest Diagnostics for supervised telehealth testing. The COVID-19 infrastructure established employer distribution relationships and regulatory credibility in at-home diagnostics. The company pivoted to GLP-1 weight management as GLP-1 demand from employers accelerated post-2023. Linda Yaccarino (former X/Twitter CEO) joined as CEO in August 2025, and Tom Brady was named Chief Wellness Officer, providing consumer brand reach.

## Method

Employees enroll via a streamlined intake, complete an at-home lab screening, and are assessed by a licensed clinician for GLP-1 eligibility based on blood results, medical history, and risk factors. Eligible patients receive FDA-approved GLP-1 prescriptions delivered home or via local pharmacy. eMed's agentic AI monitors adherence patterns and proactively intervenes at the highest churn-risk moments: early weeks when side effects peak, and plateaus when motivation dips. Employers pay a capitated model ($25 per covered life per month); enrolled employees pay $99 per month covering clinical services and medication.

## TAM logic

The US employer-sponsored health benefits market is estimated at $1T+ in annual spend. GLP-1 drugs are among the fastest-growing employer pharmacy costs, with Goldman Sachs projecting the obesity drug market at $150B by 2030. eMed targets the employer decision-maker who must contain GLP-1 costs while demonstrating health outcomes. The $150B figure reflects projected GLP-1 market size rather than an independently verified eMed TAM claim.

## Validated by

$200M Series A raised March 2026 at $2B+ valuation, led by AON Consulting. Named investors include Tom Brady, Linda Yaccarino, Jeff Aronin, Antonio Gracias, and Joe Lonsdale. Company claims 90%+ member adherence (vs. ~40-50% industry norm), 21-24 lb average weight loss, 96% reduction in pre-diabetes markers within 6 months. AON Consulting partnership announced to offer eMed as employee benefit. Collaboration announced with NIH (COVID era) and Quest Diagnostics validates clinical infrastructure pedigree.

## Competes with

Calibrate (GLP-1 weight management, pivoted away from medication in 2023), Ro (direct-to-consumer GLP-1 prescribing), Hims & Hers (compounded GLP-1), WeightWatchers (acquired Sequence), Noom Med (GLP-1 prescribing added to behavior change), Found Health, and employer-facing GLP-1 benefit managers (Vertex Pharmaceuticals Vida Health, Omada Health).

## Complements

Pharmacy benefit managers (PBMs) for drug cost management, employer benefits platforms (Aon, Mercer) for distribution, CGM companies (Dexcom) for metabolic monitoring, and primary care platforms for non-GLP-1 clinical overflow.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Employer HR decision-makers, PBMs, GLP-1 manufacturers (Novo Nordisk, Eli Lilly)
**Upstream of:** Long-term metabolic health management, primary care for comorbidities, specialist referral for high-risk patients

## Gaps

The adherence claim (90%+) is a central marketing assertion but no peer-reviewed published trial validates it at scale. The $200M Series A is the first disclosed institutional round, and the company's prior total funding appears to be under $25M, meaning the valuation jump to $2B+ is steep relative to disclosed revenue. The CEO transition to Linda Yaccarino is recent (August 2025) and the GLP-1 employer benefits space is rapidly crowding. Compounded GLP-1 market disruption (compounders losing FDA authorization) could help branded-drug programs like eMed but also creates regulatory complexity. Capitated pricing model viability depends on actuarially verifiable outcomes data.

## Notes

eMed's origin in COVID at-home testing is an unusual founder story for a GLP-1 platform. The infrastructure built for supervised at-home diagnostics (lab integration, telehealth credentialing, employer distribution) transferred directly to the GLP-1 use case. The AON lead investment is strategic: Aon is a major employee benefits broker, giving eMed instant distribution into Aon's corporate client network. Tom Brady as Chief Wellness Officer and Linda Yaccarino as CEO are consumer brand plays more than operational hires, suggesting the strategy leans heavily on employer marketing and media reach.
