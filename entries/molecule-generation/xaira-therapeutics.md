## One-liner

AI-native drug discovery and development company combining machine learning, data generation, and therapeutic product development to build new medicines.

## Problem

Drug discovery is slow, expensive, and fails at over 90% rate in clinical development because the biology-molecule-patient relationship is too complex to navigate empirically.

## Workaround

Traditional pharma R&D, CRO-based discovery, legacy HTS screening, structure-based design, and iterative medicinal chemistry.

## Entry wedge

Founded with $1B Series A (one of the largest pre-product biotech raises ever) by Hal Barron (former Chief Medical Officer, GSK and Alphabet/Verily) and Marc Tessier-Lavigne (former President of Stanford and Rockefeller). Entry strategy is to build a fully integrated AI drug discovery and development engine from scratch, rather than licensing a platform.

## Method

Integrates generative ML, large-scale in-house data generation, and computational biology to design, test, and develop therapeutics across multiple modalities from target to clinic.

## TAM logic

Targets the full pharma R&D market (~$250B globally) with the argument that a fully AI-native approach can compress the timeline and cost of discovery and clinical development. (Source: xaira.com)

## Validated by

$1B Series A from ARCH Venture Partners, F-Prime Capital, Foresite Capital, NEA, and others (2024). Leadership team includes former heads of R&D at GSK and president-level scientists. No public clinical programs yet.

## Competes with

Isomorphic Labs, insitro, Recursion, Insilico Medicine, Generate Biomedicines, all pursuing AI-native drug discovery with owned pipelines.

## Complements

CROs for wet-lab validation, genomic data providers, clinical trial infrastructure, CDMOs for manufacturing.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Target identification research, disease biology, human genetics
**Upstream of:** IND-enabling studies, clinical trials, regulatory approval

## Gaps

Pre-clinical stage, no clinical validation of AI-native approach yet. Large capital base creates execution pressure to show programs in clinic.

## Notes

The $1B launch round signals that the venture community is betting on an AI-native drug company at a scale previously reserved for clinical-stage biotechs. One of the most watched early-stage bets in the space. Marc Tessier-Lavigne is co-founder, Chairman, and CEO; Hal Barron is a co-founder. Some data providers (Crunchbase, Tracxn) label the $1B launch round Series D; the entry retains series-a pending a clearer primary source. No new financing round disclosed as of June 2026.
