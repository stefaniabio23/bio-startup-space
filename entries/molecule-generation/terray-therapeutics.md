## One-liner

Small-molecule discovery company whose tNova platform generates the world's largest experimental chemistry dataset and trains generative AI on it.

## Problem

AI models for small-molecule design are starved of high-quality quantitative binding data; public chemistry datasets are small, noisy, and inconsistent, capping model accuracy.

## Workaround

Traditional high-throughput screening, DNA-encoded libraries, structure-based design, and modelling on sparse public chemistry data.

## Entry wedge

Built proprietary nanoscale experimental chemistry hardware that measures billions of target-ligand interactions quantitatively, then used that data advantage to attract pharma partnerships (Gilead, Odyssey) and fund an internal immunology pipeline.

## Method

tNova integrates ultra-high-throughput miniaturised chemical experimentation, automation, and generative AI in a closed loop. The platform has generated 5bn+ quantitatively measured target-ligand interactions (roughly 50x all publicly available chemistry data), which train models to design and optimise small molecules for both internal and partnered programs.

## TAM logic

Targets the global small-molecule pharma R&D market (~$250B annual R&D spend). The data-generation moat is positioned as an enabling layer that improves hit rates across many programs, monetised through both owned assets and multi-target partnerships.

## Validated by

$120M Series B (Oct 2024, co-led by Bedford Ridge Capital and NVIDIA NVentures; total equity over $200M). Gilead multi-target research collaboration (Dec 2024, undisclosed upfront plus milestones and royalties across Gilead-selected targets). Odyssey Therapeutics collaboration in immunology. Internal immunology/autoimmune pipeline in preclinical development.

## Competes with

Iambic Therapeutics, Genesis Molecular AI, Schrodinger, Recursion, Insilico Medicine, and DNA-encoded library players such as X-Chem.

## Complements

Pharma partners that select targets and license candidates, CROs for downstream assays, CDMOs for manufacturing, and target-discovery companies upstream.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Target identification, disease biology, structural biology databases.
**Upstream of:** Preclinical development, IND-enabling studies, clinical trials at partners or internally.

## Gaps

No publicly disclosed clinical-stage asset yet; the data moat is proven for discovery but not yet for clinical success. Hardware-heavy model is capital intensive to scale.

## Notes

Founded 2018 by Jacob Berlin (CEO) and Eli Berlin; Monrovia, California. Company states over $200M total equity; some aggregators cite ~$226M. No public valuation disclosed for the Series B. Private company, no confirmed ticker.
