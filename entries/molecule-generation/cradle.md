## One-liner

AI protein-engineering platform that helps teams generate improved protein candidates using their own experimental data.

## Problem

Protein engineering is slow and inefficient because directed evolution requires many experimental cycles to find improved variants across large sequence spaces.

## Workaround

Directed evolution (random mutagenesis + screening), rational design by structural biology experts, internal ML teams at large pharma, CRO protein-engineering services.

## Entry wedge

SaaS platform for biotech wet-lab teams who already have experimental data but lack ML infrastructure. Entry was through small and mid-size biotechs running enzyme and antibody engineering programs who could not build internal ML teams.

## Method

Trains protein ML models on a customer's own assay data (sequence-function pairs), learns the fitness landscape, and generates improved variant candidates prioritised by predicted function — closing the loop between ML and wet-lab in days rather than months.

## TAM logic

Positions against protein engineering spend in pharma and biotech R&D. The protein engineering market is a subset of the biologics discovery segment. No explicit TAM cited; implicit target is any team running iterative protein improvement campaigns. (Source: cradle.bio)

## Validated by

$73M raised (Series B led by Bessemer Venture Partners, 2024). Published case studies across enzyme engineering, antibody affinity maturation, and stability improvement. Customers include biotech companies across Europe and the US.

## Competes with

Latent Labs, Chai Discovery (on antibody design), internal ML teams at Genentech/AstraZeneca, Protein Design Labs, Evozyne, academic tools (EVmutation, ESM-based models).

## Complements

EvolutionaryScale (ESM representations as input features), CROs for wet-lab validation, synthetic biology platforms, CDMOs for scale-up.

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Experimental protein assay data generated by customer wet-lab teams
**Upstream of:** Candidate selection for preclinical development, lead optimisation

## Gaps

Dependent on customers having existing experimental data to train on; does not generate data in-house. Platform value is bounded by the quality and quantity of customer assay data.

## Notes

Amsterdam-headquartered. One of the clearest examples of the "bring your own data" protein ML model — complementary to companies like EvolutionaryScale that provide pre-trained foundation models. The customer data flywheel is the key moat argument.
