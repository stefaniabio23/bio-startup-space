## One-liner

Makes GammaTile, an FDA-cleared bioabsorbable collagen implant embedded with cesium-131 radiation seeds that is placed in a brain tumor cavity at surgery, delivering immediate targeted radiation to residual cancer cells and dramatically cutting recurrence rates.

## Problem

After surgical removal of a brain tumor, residual cancer cells in the resection cavity cause the majority of tumor recurrences. Conventional post-surgical radiation (stereotactic radiosurgery, SRS) requires a separate procedure weeks later, creating a treatment gap during which cancer cells regrow and makes dose targeting to the cavity difficult.

## Workaround

Stereotactic radiosurgery (SRS, Gamma Knife, CyberKnife) delivered 4-6 weeks post-surgery is the current standard of care for brain metastases. Whole brain radiation therapy (WBRT) is used in some cases but causes significant cognitive side effects. For glioblastoma, temozolomide chemotherapy plus external beam radiation is standard.

## Entry wedge

FDA 510(k) clearance for recurrent intracranial neoplasms gave GT Medical an initial commercial pathway. Early adoption at academic neurosurgery centers provided outcome data and clinical champions. Expansion to community neurosurgical centers followed the ROADS Phase 3 trial design, which created reimbursement credibility with payers and gave sales teams a clinical narrative built on controlled evidence.

## Method

GammaTile is a postage-stamp-sized (2cm x 2cm) bioabsorbable collagen implant containing cesium-131 radiation seeds. At the end of tumor resection surgery, the neurosurgeon lines the tumor cavity with multiple GammaTile implants. Cesium-131 emits low-energy radiation that travels only a short distance, maximizing dose to the cavity margin while sparing surrounding healthy brain. The collagen tile dissolves naturally over time; no second procedure is required to remove the device. The product is indicated for both newly diagnosed and recurrent intracranial neoplasms. Currently adopted by 150+ centers across the U.S.

## TAM logic

Approximately 100,000+ patients undergo brain tumor surgery annually in the U.S. (primary and metastatic). GammaTile targets the subset with operable tumors, primarily the brain metastases segment (the largest by volume). With a per-procedure device price and growing reimbursement, the U.S. addressable market has been estimated at $1-3B; international expansion would add additional addressable population. The figure above is an estimate based on market size reporting; no formal TAM statement was found on the company website.

## Validated by

ROADS Phase 3 randomized controlled trial results presented at ASCO 2026: 12-month surgical bed recurrence rate 1.3% for GammaTile vs 15.4% for standard of care; 24-month overall survival 61.7% (GammaTile) vs 35.7% (SoC). $100M Series E completed June 2026, led by Viking Global Investors with MVM Partners, Gilde Healthcare, Evidity Health Capital, Medtech Venture Partners, FemHealth Ventures. Prior: $53M Series D (2025, Evidity Health Capital lead), $45M Series C (2023, Gilde Healthcare lead), $16M Series B (2020, MVM Partners lead). 2,000th patient treatment milestone announced 2025. $35M venture loan from Horizon Technology Finance (2024).

## Competes with

Stereotactic radiosurgery platforms (Elekta Gamma Knife, Accuray CyberKnife, Varian Radiosurgery), Brainlab's radiosurgery planning systems, traditional brachytherapy implants, external beam radiation oncology.

## Complements

Neurosurgical navigation systems (Brainlab, Stryker), intraoperative imaging (Zeiss Kinevo, Leica), post-surgical imaging (MRI follow-up), neuro-oncology chemotherapy protocols (temozolomide for GBM patients).

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Neurosurgery tumor resection procedure, neuro-oncology treatment planning, payer coverage decisions for brachytherapy CPT codes
**Upstream of:** Post-surgical MRI surveillance, follow-up neuro-oncology appointments, systemic therapy for remaining metastatic disease

## Gaps

The current commercial footprint (150+ centers, 2,000 patients treated by mid-2025) is small relative to the potential market. Reimbursement coverage is not universal; payer-by-payer coverage determination is a major commercial bottleneck. ROADS trial enrolled newly diagnosed operable brain metastases; evidence for recurrent disease and GBM relies on earlier, smaller studies with less statistical power. Cesium-131 production is a radioactive isotope manufacturing constraint. International expansion is undisclosed. Per Langoe (CEO) joined after earlier leadership; execution continuity risk is present.
