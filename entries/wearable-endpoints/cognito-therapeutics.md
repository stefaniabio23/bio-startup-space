## One-liner

Late clinical-stage medical device company developing Spectris, a physician-prescribed at-home wearable that delivers 40 Hz light and sound stimulation to slow cognitive decline in Alzheimer's disease.

## Problem

Alzheimer's disease has no approved therapy that durably slows cognitive decline for the majority of patients. Approved amyloid-targeting antibodies (lecanemab, donanemab) require biweekly infusions, carry serious safety risks (ARIA), and are inaccessible to most patients. Non-pharmacological approaches have had no clinical-grade alternatives.

## Workaround

Rivastigmine, donepezil, memantine (symptomatic relief only). Biogen/Eisai lecanemab (Leqembi) and Lilly donanemab (Kisunla) for eligible early AD patients — both require regular IV infusion and MRI monitoring. Caregiver support and cognitive engagement programs.

## Entry wedge

Phase 2 OVERTURE feasibility study showing a 76% reduction in cognitive decline, 77% reduction in functional decline, and 69% reduction in whole brain atrophy at six months — results striking enough to justify a pivotal trial and attract $198M in cumulative funding.

## Method

Spectris delivers synchronized light (40 Hz flickering LEDs, opaque glasses) and sound (40 Hz auditory tones, headphones) stimulation for one hour daily. This technique is called GENUS (Gamma Entrainment Using Sensory Stimuli), developed at MIT by Li-Huei Tsai and Ed Boyden. In mouse models, GENUS reduced amyloid plaques and tau tangles, improved microglial function, enhanced synaptic activity, and promoted glymphatic clearance of neurotoxic waste. Phase 2 data showed combining visual and auditory stimulation generates stronger and more widespread gamma oscillations than either modality alone.

The HOPE trial (pivotal) is a double-blind, sham-controlled study of 670 patients (MMSE 15-28, mild to moderate AD) across 60+ US sites, aiming for topline results in 2026. FDA Breakthrough Device Designation was granted in 2021.

## TAM logic

Approximately 6.9 million Americans have Alzheimer's; globally approximately 55 million have dementia. If Spectris receives FDA clearance and captures even 10-15% of the mild-to-moderate AD population at a $3-5K/year price point, the annual revenue opportunity exceeds $2-4B in the US alone. The at-home, prescription device model avoids infusion center costs and could achieve rapid uptake compared to biologic alternatives. (Source: Alzheimer's Association data; company investor materials)

## Validated by

$105M Series C (March 2026, oversubscribed, led by Morningside Ventures, with IAG Capital Partners, Starbloom Capital, New Vintage, Apollo Health Ventures, Benvolio Group). Prior Series B: $73M (2023). Total funding approximately $198M. FDA Breakthrough Device Designation (2021). Phase 2 OVERTURE: positive across cognitive, functional, and structural (brain atrophy) endpoints. Phase 3 HOPE trial: fully enrolled as of 2025, topline results expected 2026. Data presented at AAIC 2025.

## Competes with

Lecanemab (Leqembi, Eisai/Biogen) and donanemab (Kisunla, Eli Lilly) as the primary amyloid-targeting competitors. Prothena, AC Immune, and other tau-targeting programs. Broader neuromodulation competitors: Neuronics, Electric Mind. Non-pharmacological cognitive interventions (BrainHQ, Lumosity) are not direct competitors but compete for patient attention. No direct competitor has a late-stage sensory stimulation device for AD.

## Complements

Neurologists and geriatric psychiatrists prescribing Alzheimer's therapies, memory care clinics, caregiver support platforms, biomarker diagnostics for early AD staging (CSF amyloid/tau, PET imaging, blood-based biomarkers from companies like C2N, ALZpath).

## Value chain position

**Downstream of:** Alzheimer's diagnosis (clinical assessment, biomarker confirmation), prescription by neurologist
**Upstream of:** Caregiver monitoring, cognitive testing follow-up, MRI monitoring for disease progression

## Gaps

The HOPE trial is the pivotal moment — if topline results are negative or weaker than Phase 2, the company's entire value is at risk. Phase 2 to Phase 3 failure rates in AD have been catastrophically high historically. FDA clearance path for a device that claims disease modification (not just symptom management) is novel and uncertain. Reimbursement by Medicare/payers for an at-home neurostimulation device is not guaranteed even with clearance. Patient compliance with one hour/day of device use is an adoption risk.

## Notes

Founded by Emery N. Brown and others; scientific lineage directly from MIT (Li-Huei Tsai lab). HQ: Lexington, MA. The GENUS approach is based on a 2016 Nature paper from the Tsai/Boyden labs at MIT showing 40 Hz flickering light reduced amyloid in mouse models. Spectris would represent the first device-based neuroprotective therapy for Alzheimer's if cleared — a category that does not currently exist.
