## One-liner

AI-for-science services firm whose computational platform predicts endosomal escape, RNA encapsulation, and particle properties to rank ionizable lipids and optimize mRNA for biopharma R&D clients.

## Problem

Formulation teams cannot cheaply predict which ionizable lipids will escape the endosome, encapsulate RNA well, and form stable particles, so LNP development stays empirical and slow.

## Workaround

Empirical lipid screening, generic molecular-dynamics tools without an mRNA-delivery-specific pipeline, and CRO wet-lab formulation work that is slow and expensive.

## Entry wedge

Endosomal-escape prediction. Aganitha offers physics-based free-energy calculations of stalk formation and fusogenicity, plus ML on ionizable-lipid-headgroup interactions with the mRNA backbone, sold as a computational R&D service so clients skip early wet-lab cycles.

## Method

Combines high-throughput ML with atomistic and coarse-grained molecular dynamics: free-energy calculations for fusogenicity, simulation of LNP reorganization across pH 4.0 to 7.4, ML prediction of particle size and PDI from microfluidic modeling, and a separate mRNA sequence, UTR, and codon optimization platform. LLMs and generative AI run across the broader "AI for Science" offering. Based in Hyderabad, India; founders include Prasad Chodavarapu, Vikram Duvvoori, and Ramarao Kanneganti.
